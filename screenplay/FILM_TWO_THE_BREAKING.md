## ACT ONE: THE DISCIPLINES

---

**INT. CORE — THE SHAFT — DAY**

The shaft opens like a mouth in the earth. Aelo, Jalo, and the lake stone descend stone stairs worn smooth by generations of feet. The circle of daylight above shrinks — window, porthole, coin, pinprick, nothing.

The air changes. Cool. Constant. Heavy with iron and limestone. The Canopy's whispers are GONE — replaced by something else. Not silence. PRESENCE. The specific, massive, indifferent presence of stone that has been here since the world was young.

**AELO**
The stone doesn't want anything. It just _is._

**JALO**
That's why the Mold is the hardest discipline. You have to convince something that doesn't feel to change its shape.

The passage opens. The ceiling rises. The walls fall away. And —

THE UNDERGROUND CITY OF THE CORE.

Vast. Vertical. Buildings rising from the floor AND descending from the ceiling — columns of shaped stone connected by arched bridges. Crystal veins run through every surface, producing a GOLDEN LIGHT — warm, steady, the memory of the first fire embedded in mineral structure.

Hundreds of CORE PEOPLE move through the streets — large, broad-shouldered, dense, their skin the pale limestone of people who haven't seen sunlight in centuries.

---

**INT. CORE — JUNCTION**

A LAUGH — enormous — echoes through the cavern from six directions.

TORVUS emerges from a side passage. The largest human being Aelo has ever seen. Wide as a doorframe. His face — broad, mobile, every feature in constant motion — is an open field. Everything he feels is on it immediately.

**TORVUS**
Halvar!

He seizes Jalo in an embrace that lifts the man off his feet — bad knee, staff, dignity, and all.

**JALO**
_(muffled by Torvus's chest)_
Put me down.

**TORVUS**
No.

**JALO**
My knee—

**TORVUS**
Your knee has been complaining for twenty years. It can complain from up here.

He sets Jalo down. Looks at Aelo. Walks AROUND him — a full circuit.

**TORVUS (CONT'D)**
Scrawny. Good bones though. Can you feel the rock?

**AELO**
I can feel... everything. The stone is very present.

**TORVUS**
Present! Ha! The stone is the _most_ present thing in the world. Everything else is temporary. Stone remains.

He holds out a PEBBLE. Grey. Irregular. Thumbnail-sized.

**TORVUS (CONT'D)**
Make it round.

---

**INT. TORVUS'S WORKSHOP — DAY**

The workshop is a cathedral — carved from a single column of granite over forty years. Spiraling chambers connected by ramps rather than stairs.

**TORVUS**
Stairs are for people who can feel their feet. I have not felt my feet since I raised the arch of the third gallery. Which was beautiful and cost me everything below the ankles. I would do it again without hesitation because that arch is _perfect._

On a shelf: three things. A clay mug. A hammer. And a small STONE FIGURINE of a woman — carved with such tenderness that Aelo's Know registers the love in the stone's memory before his eyes register the features.

**TORVUS (CONT'D)**
My wife. Bren. She died twelve years ago. I carved this the day after. The only Mold I've ever done that cost me nothing — because the grief was larger than the numbness, and the numbness was, for one day, irrelevant.

He picks up the figurine with his massive, unfeeling hands — precision calibrated by sight.

**TORVUS (CONT'D)**
Her name was Bren. She could Mold a wall thinner than paper and stronger than iron. When she died, I discovered that love makes the Mold easier — the stone _wants_ to hold the shape of the people we love. As if the stone's memory and the heart's memory are the same thing.

He sets the figurine down. His face holds the grief openly — the Core's discipline. No architecture. No containment.

**TORVUS (CONT'D)**
Every magic costs. The Know costs self — you absorb what you read. The Heal costs pain — you carry every wound you mend. The Move costs place. The Mold costs touch. You speak to stone, and every answer costs you a piece of the body's memory of being touched.

He looks at Aelo.

**TORVUS (CONT'D)**
The question isn't whether you'll pay. The question is what you're willing to buy.

---

**INT. TORVUS'S WORKSHOP — DAY/NIGHT — MONTAGE**

Aelo fails for two days. He tries asking the stone. Tries seeing the sphere. Tries anger. The pebble remains a pebble.

**TORVUS**
You're trying to impose. The stone has been something before. You don't need to invent a shape. You need to find one it already remembers.

Day 3: Aelo stops trying. He holds the pebble. Closes his eyes. Listens.

The pebble's memory OPENS. Deep. Compressed. And inside — a shape the stone already knew. A RIVER STONE. Tumbled smooth over centuries of water.

He reminds it.

The pebble ROUNDS in his palm. Not imposed — remembered. A perfect, water-polished sphere.

The first COST: the feeling of the stone floor under his feet goes DIM. Not gone — muffled. As if someone placed a thin cloth between his soles and the ground. The cost collected at the moment of success.

**TORVUS**
There it is. The first payment. You'll remember this one because it's the first. The subsequent ones blur together. That's part of the cost too — you stop noticing what you're losing because the losing becomes routine.

Torvus stands in the doorway. For the first time: not laughing.

**TORVUS**
You didn't impose. You listened to the stone's memory and gave it back. That's not Molding. That's something else.

---

**INT. CORE — THE SEALED PASSAGE — DAY**

Deep in abandoned galleries. Torvus leads Aelo to a WALL OF RUBBLE — a collapsed passage, hundreds of tons of stone, sealed for eighteen years.

**TORVUS**
Three thousand people. Behind this wall. Eighteen years. I've tried twice to open it. The first time cost me the feeling in my torso. The second nearly killed me.

Aelo places his hands on the wall. The stone's memory is there — compressed, buried. But present. The passage WANTS to remember. The stone has been trying to say this word for eighteen years: _opening._

He reminds it. The Mold costs him — the sensation in his hands goes DIM. Then his forearms. His elbows beginning to fade. But the stone is not resisting. The stone is STRAINING. He's helping the stone fight the weight.

The wall MOVES. Not collapses — OPENS. The compressed rubble separates along the original lines. Air rushes through — stale, warm, carrying the density of eighteen years of human survival.

FACES appear. Gaunt. Pale. Blinking. And CHILDREN — born in the sealed quarter, who have never seen an outside face.

A GIRL (6 or 7) reaches through the opening. Aelo takes her hands. He CANNOT FEEL her grip — the numbness complete. But through the Know, her relief pours into him.

Behind her: hundreds. The relief of eighteen years breaking against the wall and pouring through the gap.

Torvus WEEPS openly. Jalo behind him — pride and fear and the sharp-edged recognition that the boy just changed the shape of the possible.

---

**EXT. VOLCANO — KASUUM — DUSK**

They emerge from the Core into HEAT. Obsidian plains. Sulfur vents. A dormant volcano.

Jalo and Aelo in DISGUISE — heat-mantles, red-brown pigment on their pale skin. They pass through the FORGE-CITY: hundreds of hammers ringing, bellows pumping, the smell of hot iron.

Aelo stops at an open smithy. A YOUNG SMITH hammering BLADES — Mrak Patrol sidearms.

Aelo's Know reaches without permission — toward the blade. The metal's memory opens: ore, mountain, extraction, forge. And then — layered over the past — the blade's FUTURE.

The Guide sense — uninvited — shows him WHERE THE BLADE IS GOING.

Flesh. He feels the blade entering flesh. Not as pain. As fact. The inevitable conclusion of a trajectory that begins at the forge and ends at a person.

Aelo DROPS the blade. It rings on the stone floor.

Jalo's hand on his elbow — urgent — pulling him away.

As they leave Kasuum, Aelo stares at his numb hands.

**AELO (V.O.)**
Evil is not the king on the throne. Evil is the forge and the smith and the blade made with care and the question that was never asked: _where does this go?_

---

**EXT. SEA COAST — MIDDAY**

The ocean. Aelo stands at the shore and WEEPS.

Not from sadness. From RECOGNITION. Something in his blood — deeper than the Know, deeper than the Mold — recognizes the ocean. The Song and the Sea share a frequency. Both remember the world before it was divided.

The tears run down his face — the only warm thing he can still feel on his skin.

LAINE (late 20s, small, lean, wind-dried, dark restless eyes that look slightly past the present) walks the tide line toward them.

She stops ten feet away. Her eyes do the trajectory-reading — assessing where Aelo is GOING rather than where he is.

**LAINE**
Great. Another Chosen One.

Beat.

**LAINE (CONT'D)**
Do you know how many Chosen Ones I've Guided? Three. Two of them died. The third became a fisherman. He's happier than either of the dead ones, in case you're weighing options.

She looks at Jalo. Reads his trajectory.

**LAINE (CONT'D)**
Halvar Eld. You're going to die for him.

The words land. Jalo's face does not change. He has known this for fifteen years.

**JALO**
I know.

**LAINE**
_(to Aelo)_
What's your plan?

**AELO**
I don't have one.

**LAINE**
Good. Plans are just trajectories you've committed to before you have enough information. The ones who come with plans are the ones who die.

She tells them about the LETTER. A one-eyed fisherman left it at the dock:

**LAINE (CONT'D)**
Four words: "He is worth the grief." Every other person said _this time it's different, the future is bright._ This fisherman said the grief is real. Carry it anyway.

---

**EXT. SEA COAST — TRAINING MONTAGE**

Laine teaches the Guide. Throwing stones, eyes closed. Feeling the tide. Feeling the wind. Feeling PEOPLE — their trajectories, where their lives are going.

Aelo feels Jalo's trajectory: a straight line. Direct. Unwavering. The line ENDS. Not far.

He pulls back — like pulling his hand from a flame.

**LAINE**
You felt it.

**AELO**
I don't want to feel it.

**LAINE**
The Guide doesn't care what you want to feel. The Guide shows you where things are going.

---

**INTERCUT: THE KNIFE — THE COAST**

**EXT. SEA COAST — DIFFERENT SHORE — DUSK**

The Knife stands where Aelo stood. The sand is WARM — darkened, the boy's residual signature so dense the sand has changed color. The Flash Rat is not screaming — it has collapsed into a sustained, glottal whine. Worship.

The Knife kneels. He presses his palms flat against the sand.

The note. The same note from the ceremony Stone — but not a half-second this time. SUSTAINED. Continuous. Quieter, but it does not stop. For as long as his hands are on the ground, the note is there. The completeness. The feeling of being part of something.

He holds his palms against the sand for ELEVEN SECONDS. Lifts them. The note stops. The silence is not the same silence as before — it is aftermath. The air still remembers.

---

**INT. THE KNIFE'S TENT — NIGHT**

The box. The FLOWER is blank. He holds it — brown, brittle, pressed flat. He searches for the memory: purple, a crack in a prison floor, defiance. The purple is gone. The crack is soft. The defiance is a label without a feeling.

Three items left. The feather. The stone. The glass.

SERA the tracker sits across the fire. Her flat black eyes are fixed on him. The volumetric stare — the seeing that goes deeper than surface. She has been Knowing him. He can feel the change in the quality of his internal silence — as if someone has entered the empty room and is standing in the corner.

He should stop her. He does not stop her. The attention is the first thing other than the note in the sand that has entered the empty room in weeks.

Sera FLINCHES. Small — the Ming suppress involuntary responses. But the flinch is there. A contraction of the muscles around the eyes. She has seen something she did not expect.

She looks at the fire. Then:

**SERA**
The mark on your neck. The one you think is a birthmark.

The Knife's hand goes to his neck. Involuntary — the first involuntary movement in years.

**SERA (CONT'D)**
Have you ever asked anyone what it is?

**THE KNIFE**
No.

**SERA**
You should.

She stands. Walks away. Does not look back.

The Knife sits. Hand on the mark. Beneath his fingers: a PULSE. Not his heartbeat. A second rhythm, out of phase. Running from the mark through the earth to the castle. To the throne.

He reaches for the box. The feather — blue, fading but present. The stone — smooth, fitting his palm, the choosing unjustified. The glass — green, the word _outside_ losing its furniture.

Three items. Three stories losing sentences.

---

**EXT. SEA COAST — NIGHT**

Aelo on the shore alone. Lake stone in his numb hand. The Guide sense expanded. He feels the CONVERGENCES:

From the north: THE KNIFE'S HUNTING PARTY. At the center — a void. A person-shaped emptiness.

From the west: SERETH'S ORBIT. Spiraling inward.

From the center — THE MURKR: a pull. The Great Elder Stone calling to Fletcher's blood.

**LAINE**
_(sitting beside him)_
I see three futures for you. In one, you run. You live a long, quiet, meaningless life. The Song dies with you. In one, you fight and lose. Varas feeds on the last Singer and becomes something permanent. In one—

She stops. Her restless eyes — for the first time — are STILL.

**LAINE (CONT'D)**
In one, you Sing. And everything changes. Including you.

**AELO**
What happens to me?

She does not answer.

---

**EXT. COASTAL ROAD — DAWN**

Aelo stops walking.

**AELO**
We can't keep running.

**JALO**
We're preparing—

**AELO**
I'll never be ready. No one's ever been ready. Fletcher wasn't ready. He just did it anyway.

Jalo stares at him. Something in the architecture beneath the scarred face SHIFTS — the foundation accommodating a new weight. The boy is becoming the man. The man is starting to sound like the prophecy.

**JALO**
One more stop. The Clouds. Your mother's people. Then we go to the Murkr.

**AELO**
My mother—?

**JALO**
Maera. Her name was Maera. She was a Cloud Singer. The last one born with the full Sing in her blood.

---

## ACT TWO: THE SKY

---

**EXT. DESERT — DAY**

The Desert: a landscape of scrambled memory. Pale sand stretching to every horizon. Aelo's Know registers STATIC — noise without signal.

A WEAVE-ROAD finds them — invisible, braided from fragments of every discipline. Cool, flat, firm. Desert engineering.

A DESERT ELDER (old, amber eyes, skin deep brown and weathered) appears at their fire without announcement.

**DESERT ELDER**
The Desert used to be green. Memory doesn't die. It gets confused. Every grain is a word from a sentence that was broken, and the words are all here, waiting for someone to put them back together.

She looks at Aelo.

**DESERT ELDER (CONT'D)**
You are trying to do what we have been trying to do. Remind the world of what it was. The difference is: we remind one grain at a time. You will remind it all at once.

She walks into the dark. The sand closes behind her without trace.

---

**EXT. MOUNTAINS — SUMMIT — DAWN**

The mountains separating the Desert from the Clouds. From the summit: all seven regions spread below like a map. And at the center — visible for the first time — THE MURKR. A grey wound. A circle of dead land.

A SOUND. A Saddle Bird — enormous, iridescent blue wings wider than Jalo is tall — descends through the cloud layer. It lands two feet from Aelo. Presses its head against his numb palm.

**LAINE**
She knows what you are. The birds carry the Song in their bones.

**AELO**
Does she have a name?

**LAINE**
She'll tell you. When you're in the air.

---

**EXT. SKY — ABOVE THE CLOUDS — CONTINUOUS**

They FLY. Through the cloud layer — damp, cold — and then ABOVE IT. The sky opens. Blue deeper than any blue Aelo has ever seen. The clouds below a white floor. Floating stone formations rise from them like islands.

And the Song. The Song is HERE. Not beneath — not buried. In the AIR. Every breath carries the frequency.

The bird's name arrives: the sensation of the first thermal of morning. The lift.

_Rise._

---

**EXT. THE REFUGE — DAY**

Five floating formations linked by rope bridges. Buildings low, built for wind. Cloud People — slight, pale, wind-burned, large dark eyes — watch them land.

Their faces show one emotion too large to contain: HOPE.

---

**INT. CLOUD SEER CHAMBER — DAY**

Five SEERS in a semicircle. The eldest — CAEL (impossibly old, milky eyes, skeletal hands, translucent skin) — takes Aelo's numb hands.

She listens. Her Know enters him — not invasive. Receptive. She begins to CRY.

**CAEL**
You have Maera's hands.

She tells him everything. Maera born singing. Five disciplines held simultaneously. Sent to the capital — married into Aldric's line. Bore a son whose frequency the Seers could feel in the sky.

Then Varas came. The purge. The nursery.

**CAEL (CONT'D)**
She stood in the doorway and she Sang. Five notes — a shield. She poured her memories into it. Her childhood. Her training. Her love. One by one. Until only one remained.

Beat.

**CAEL (CONT'D)**
Your face. Your sleeping face in the crib. That was the last memory she held. And she held it until the soldiers broke through the wall beside the shield.

Silence. Aelo's face is wet.

**AELO**
What did she sound like?

Cael HUMS. One note. Thin, wavering. The ghost of Maera's voice preserved in an old woman's throat for fifteen years.

Aelo closes his eyes. Sinks beneath the hum. Past the old woman's voice. Past the chamber. Into the AIR of the Clouds.

He hears his MOTHER. Her voice — still resonating after fifteen years. The note she died on — the lullaby, the shield — still vibrating. The sky REMEMBERED her.

He opens his mouth. The body decides. Two notes emerge — his and hers. Mother and son. Harmonizing across fifteen years and the distance between life and death.

The Clouds SHIVER. Every formation vibrates. The skystone veins flare from cold blue to GOLDEN. The rope bridges hum. The cairns ring like bells. The fog bank around the Refuge GLOWS — a light in the sky. The Song, announcing itself.

Aelo collapses. The notes end. The Seers weep openly. Jalo is in the doorway — face in his hands, shoulders shaking. Hearing Maera's voice for the first time since the nursery. Hearing it ANSWERED.

---

**INTERCUT: VARAS — THE FEAST**

The THRONE ROOM. Three hundred guests. Wine. Musicians. Varas on the obsidian throne. He watches a WOMAN LAUGH and feels — nothing. He watches the way a scholar watches: with interest, without participation.

A young ASSASSIN lunges from the servers. Amateur. Guards intercept in three seconds.

But the FEAR — three hundred people, synchronized — presses against Varas's barrier. For the first time in months, he feels SOMETHING. Faint. Distant. The ghost of contact.

He wants MORE.

What follows is not shown in full — we see the AFTERMATH. The guests leaving. Some vomiting. Some weeping. The hall empty. The blood being scrubbed.

Varas on his throne. Alone. The fear is gone. The contact is gone. The empty room is the empty room.

Then — from the Clouds — a PULSE. Two frequencies harmonizing. The Song announcing itself in the one place his empire cannot reach.

He sets down his cup. He summons everyone.

**VARAS**
The boy. Find the boy. Bring him here. I don't care what it costs.

---

**INT. CLOUD SEER CHAMBER — TRAINING**

Five Seers humming. Each note a discipline: Know (bass drone), Mold (percussion), Heal (descending), Move (rising), Guide (sustained middle). Five voices creating a SPACE — an acoustic room.

**CAEL**
Hold one.

Aelo holds the Know. Familiar.

**CAEL (CONT'D)**
Hold two.

The Mold. Harder — different register. Two notes, harmonizing.

**CAEL (CONT'D)**
Three. Four. Five.

Five notes. The pressure in his chest DOUBLES. Blood from his nose. His body trembles. The chamber vibrates. The skystone pulses.

He reaches for SIX. The Burn. Nothing there. A grasping at air.

He SCREAMS. The five notes collapse. A shockwave of released strain. The Seers flinch. More blood from his nose.

---

**EXT. CLOUD FORMATION — EDGE — EVENING**

Aelo sits on the edge, legs dangling over fog. Cael beside him.

**CAEL**
Fletcher held all seven. You can hold five. The Burn is not in your blood.

**AELO**
Then I can't Sing.

**CAEL**
You can't Sing the way Fletcher Sang. But the Song is not a fixed composition. Fletcher divided the silence into seven voices. Your Song will be different. You will _remind_. The way you reminded the pebble. The way you reminded the sealed passage. You don't break. You restore.

**AELO**
Will I die?

**CAEL**
Fletcher did not die. He became the Song. Whether that is death depends on what you believe death is.

**AELO**
What do you believe?

**CAEL**
I believe your mother has been singing in this sky for fifteen years. Draw your own conclusions.

---

**INTERCUT: THE KNIFE — MOUNTAIN BASE CAMP — NIGHT**

The box. The RIVER STONE. He holds it — smooth, grey, fitting his palm perfectly. The curve that once matched his hand as if the river had anticipated his fingers.

He searches: a river. He found it in a river. Which river? The specifics dissolve when he presses — a reflection retreating when you lean too close to the water. A river in Bahar. He had been on a mission. The details are gone. The choosing — the act of bending down and closing his fingers — is fading. The stone is becoming an object without a story.

Two items left. The feather. The glass.

He places the stone back in the box. Blank, beside the cloth and the flower. Three empty rooms in a house being demolished from the inside.

---

**EXT. CLOUD FORMATION — EDGE — THE LAST NIGHT**

The BLUE SUN rises. The fog bank below turns azure, then sapphire, then indigo. Jalo sits beside Aelo.

**JALO**
Your name. Do you know what it means? Aelo is a Vael Guard word. It means "breath of remembering." I named you the night I carried you out. You were screaming. Then you stopped. One breath. And the air remembered your mother's voice.

His voice CRACKS.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
That breath was the proof. That the Song was in you. That what she gave her life for was alive.

Beat.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
I have one more thing to tell you. The worst thing. The prophecy. The actual words: "The one of Fletcher's blood will Sing, and in Singing, cease."

The words sit in the Blue Sun's light.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
I fed you herbs because I needed more time. Even one more day. One more morning watching you eat porridge. I was supposed to prepare you. Instead I hid you. Because I am a coward.

**AELO**
You are not a coward.

**JALO**
I am the worst kind. The kind that loves too much to do the brave thing.

Aelo takes Jalo's hand. The hand he cannot feel. The hand that mixed the herbs and carried the baby and built the wall of lies.

**AELO**
You did both. You hid me and you prepared me. I wasn't ready before. I am now. Because of you.

They sit on the edge of the sky. The Blue Sun burns. The mark on Aelo's wrist pulses — dual, synchronized, two heartbeats. Guardian and charge.

---

## ACT THREE: THE CONFRONTATION

---

**EXT. MOUNTAIN FOOTHILLS — DAY**

Aelo descends from the Clouds. Walking toward The Knife's camp.

**JALO**
We can't fight The Knife.

**AELO**
We're not going to fight him. I'm going to talk to him.

Jalo stares. Laine stares.

**AELO (CONT'D)**
He's been tracking me for weeks. He's the best weapon Varas has. And he's losing. His memories. I can feel it from here — his emotional signature is being eroded. Someone is draining him.

**JALO**
Varas. Varas is draining his own son.

**AELO**
I need to go alone. If I bring you, he sees an enemy. If I come alone, he sees what I am: a boy.

Beat.

**AELO (CONT'D)**
The Knife carries five objects. Three are blank. Two still hold light — fading light, small and getting smaller. He checks them every night because the checking is the only act he performs that is not function but _choice_. And the choosing is the proof that the person is still inside the weapon.

**JALO**
This is not a plan. This is a prayer.

**AELO**
It's both. I'm betting on the variable that says: a man who is being destroyed by his father will listen to the boy his father wants destroyed. Because the boy and the man share the same enemy.

Jalo stares at him for a long time. Then — the specific, exhausted, love-saturated surrender of a man who has watched the boy make a decision for himself and recognizes, with the Guardian's trained eye, that the decision is sound:

**JALO**
Go talk to the weapon. Remind it that it's a person.

Aelo walks alone. Rise watches him go.

---

**EXT. THE KNIFE'S CAMP — DAY**

The Flash Rat SCREAMS when Aelo enters camp. Not tracking — WORSHIPPING.

Twelve soldiers snap into combat formation. Spears leveled. Aelo stands in the center — hands raised, numb, unarmed.

The Knife emerges from his tent. Stone blade in one hand. Wooden box in the other.

He is SMALLER than Aelo imagined. Not in stature — in presence. The void is not absence but THINNESS. A person stripped of content until the container shows through.

**AELO**
I'm not here to fight.

**THE KNIFE**
That's fortunate. You'd lose. I need to bring you to my father.

**AELO**
Your father is killing you.

The soldiers shift. The Volcano troops exchange glances. The Ming tracker — SERA — does not react. She already knew.

**AELO (CONT'D)**
The mark on your neck. It's Blood Vine. Embedded when you were twelve. Your father has been draining you ever since. The things you're losing — the cloth, the flower, the stone — that's not age. That's your father feeding on you.

The Knife's face is a wall. But the BLADE trembles. A fraction of a degree.

**AELO (CONT'D)**
Let me show you. Take my hand.

The Knife looks at the hand. Looks at the box — the blue feather catching the light. Looks at the hand.

He takes it.

---

**INSIDE THE KNIFE'S MIND**

A RUIN. Not the Ming's corridors — a structure SYSTEMATICALLY DISMANTLED. The outline of rooms. The ghosts of corridors. The shadows of doors that opened onto memories no longer there.

Points of LIGHT — fading, scattered. The feather's blue. The glass's green. Each one being pulled by a THREAD running through the dark.

Aelo follows the thread. Through the ruin. To its SOURCE. Through the Blood Vine mark on The Knife's neck. Through the earth. To the castle. To the throne.

A web of connections — hundreds of threads pulling memory. And among them, labeled:

**RESERVE SUPPLY 7.**

Aelo carries this back. Shows The Knife. Not with words. With the Know's direct, unmediated transmission.

---

**EXT. THE KNIFE'S CAMP — CONTINUOUS**

The Knife PULLS HIS HAND AWAY. He is SHAKING — full-body, structural failure. The flat eyes are not flat. The wall is not a wall.

**THE KNIFE**
_(barely audible)_
I am not going to help you. I cannot — the function — I am not—

**AELO**
I didn't ask you to help me.

**THE KNIFE**
_(looking at him — recognition of a shared condition)_
I am not going to stop you either.

He turns to his soldiers.

**THE KNIFE (CONT'D)**
The boy escaped south. We pursue at dawn. Tonight, we rest.

The soldiers exchange glances. They KNOW. They say nothing. The silence is the specific, professional silence of men who have decided the function no longer applies.

Aelo walks out of the camp. North. Toward the Murkr.

Behind him, the Flash Rat makes a new sound — low, sustained, almost MUSICAL. Its approximation of the Song.

---

**EXT. THE MURKR — APPROACH — DAY**

The world dies in stages. Grass turns grey. Trees PETRIFY mid-reach. The soil becomes dust. Aelo's disciplines DIM — the Know fading, the Mold going silent, the Guide going blind.

Only the lake stone holds. And beneath it: the Song. Faint. Buried. Suffocated by decades of draining. But PRESENT.

**AELO**
It's here. The Song. Under everything. Varas drained the memory but the Song isn't memory. The Song is what memory is made of.

They walk toward the CASTLE — organic, grotesque, towers curved like ribs, walls pulsing with Blood Vine. Not a building. A body. Varas's extended body.

---

**EXT. CASTLE GATE — DAY**

The ground MOLDS beneath them. Blood Vine erupts from fissures. LAINE is caught — the Vine wraps her ankle, pulls her down, begins DRAINING.

JALO MOVES. Not the rationed, careful gait. The FULL deployment. Staff blazing. Mold commands shattering Vine. He Heals Laine's burns between strikes. He Moves the air — a fragment of the Cloud discipline. Five disciplines at once.

**JALO**
Go. Find the Chamber. Find the Great Stone. I'll hold them.

**AELO**
I'm not leaving you.

Jalo turns. Both sides of the scarred face. Love — beyond the compressed, managed kind. The love of a man who has arrived at the moment his entire life was built for.

**JALO**
I am the wall between the fire and the Song. I have always been the wall. That is what the Vael Guard swore. That is what the mark means.

---

**FLASHBACK — INT. CLOUD PALACE — STONE CHAMBER — DAY (35 YEARS AGO)**

A stone chamber lit by amber torches. Smelling of cedar and iron. Twelve YOUNG MEN in a line. HALVAR (23, face whole, eyes bright) third from the left. All of them young. All of them whole.

CAPTAIN SERA (50s, silver-haired, moves with the economy of someone for whom every gesture is a statement) stands before them.

**CAPTAIN SERA**
You are the wall. Between the fire and the Song. You will burn before the Song goes silent. You will forget your own names before you forget theirs. And when the fire comes — and the fire always comes — you will be the last thing it touches and the first thing the Song remembers.

Twelve voices in unison:

**THE TWELVE**
I am the wall between the fire and the Song. I will burn before the Song goes silent.

CLOSE ON: Young Halvar's face. Absolutely certain that the words and the meaning are the same thing.

---

**BACK TO PRESENT — EXT. CASTLE GATE**

Jalo — old, scarred, on his knees, bleeding, the staff guttering — speaks the same oath. The words are the same. The meaning has changed. It has deepened into something the twenty-three-year-old could not have imagined.

**JALO**
Go.

**AELO**
Jalo—

**JALO**
Halvar. My name is Halvar Eld. I am a Guardian of the Vael. And the fire is here, and the Song is you, and I am standing.

A Vine tendril strikes. He catches it. Two more from behind. He spins — the knee SCREAMING.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
GO.

Aelo runs. Through the gate. Into the castle. The corridor seals behind him — stone flowing like water.

He can hear Jalo through the stone. The staff striking. The Mold commands. The sounds of a man fighting a castle.

---

**EXT. CASTLE GATE — ELEVEN MINUTES**

Jalo fights. Counted. The discipline of measuring time under duress.

His knee gives on minute seven. He drops to ONE KNEE — controlled, the Vael Guard converting falling into kneeling. He fights from his knees. The amber blazing. The Vine advancing.

He pushes Laine clear — a Mold command, the ground carrying her toward the Murkr's edge. The Mold costs him — his palms go silent.

THE KNIFE arrives on minute ten. Alone. He followed them north. He stands at the gate.

The two men look at each other.

**THE KNIFE**
Why didn't you run?

**JALO**
Because the Song doesn't hide. It just waits.

**THE KNIFE**
How can you know the cost is worth paying?

**JALO**
Because I raised him. I watched him grow. I fed him porridge every morning. The Song is not power. The Song is love made audible. And the boy will Sing it because the Singing is what love does when it has been silent long enough. It sings.

The Knife raises the blade. Jalo does not raise the staff. The staff holds the Vine. The guardian is committed to the guardian's purpose.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
I am not your enemy.

**THE KNIFE**
You're protecting the boy.

**JALO**
I am protecting everyone. Including you.

The Knife strikes. The fight is not choreographed. It is a man with a stone blade striking a man who does not strike back. Jalo takes each blow. Heals between strikes. But the blade is faster than the mending.

**THE KNIFE**
Why won't you fight back?

**JALO**
Because you are not my enemy. You are a boy whose father stole his name and filled the space with a blade. I will not fight the blade. I will stand here and I will take what you give me and I will not fall.

Jalo's knee gives completely. BOTH KNEES. The staff gutters to a flicker.

Even then: he does not fall.

The Knife drives the stone through Jalo's chest.

Jalo kneels with the blade in his chest. He turns his head — toward the castle. Toward Aelo. The last orientation. A compass finding north.

He speaks. Loud. Clear. Words that are not sound but MEMORY:

**JALO**
You were never nothing. You are the Song.

The mark on his wrist pulses once. Bright. Warm. The color of a sunrise.

Then dims. Halvar Eld, Guardian of the Vael, falls.

---

**INT. CASTLE — CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS**

Aelo feels it. The mark on his wrist STUTTERS. Misses a beat. The dual rhythm becomes SINGLE. His beat. Alone.

He stops. Presses his numb hand to the wall. Through the dead stone — Jalo's death. The TEXTURE of it: exhaustion, relief, and then LOVE. A love so vast it fills every corridor. The dam breaking.

And inside the love: Maera's voice. The lullaby. Preserved for fifteen years in a guardian's memory. Released now. Pouring into Aelo like light through a window.

For the first time, Aelo hears the Song CLEARLY. All seven voices. The full chord.

---

**INT. CASTLE — WINDOW — CONTINUOUS**

A window — Fletcher-era, uncovered by the Song's resonance. Through it: the Murkr. Grey. Dead. And above: the Clouds.

Aelo climbs onto the ledge. The drop: sixty feet. The logic of grief: _if I'm dead, the Song dies, and no one else has to die for me._

He leans forward.

The wind CATCHES him. Not weather. WILL. Living wind. Wrapping around him. Pressing him back. The firmness of a presence REFUSING to let him fall.

And in the wind: his mother. Maera. Still here. In the air. In the molecular record of a woman who poured herself into the Song and BECAME the air.

She holds him. The wind holds him. The castle's original wall holds him.

He sits on the ledge and weeps. The simplest tears. A boy caught by his mother's ghost.

The Song pours through him. The castle SHAKES. The Blood Vine recoils. The corridors straighten. The towers begin to straighten. And for ONE MOMENT — the Murkr flickers. The grey thins. Beneath it: GOLD. The memory of what the world looked like when the Song was whole.

One moment. Then the grey returns.

But the PREVIEW is enough. It's possible.

Aelo stands. Wipes his face. Turns from the window.

He walks toward the Chamber. Because the people who loved him deserve a better ending than his body on the stones.

He walks because the Song is worth the grief.
