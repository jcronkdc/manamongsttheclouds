# Cover Design Brief — *Man Amongst the Clouds*

## For the Designer

### The Book
*Man Amongst the Clouds* is a literary fantasy novel (153,000 words) about a boy who discovers he can hear the world's memory sing, and must cross seven regions to confront a king who was born deaf to the Song and has spent seventy years consuming the world to fill the silence. It is a story about memory, sacrifice, grief, and the cost of being the kind of person who listens.

### Tone & Aesthetic
This is **literary fantasy**, not sword-and-sorcery. The cover should feel:
- **Atmospheric, not action-oriented** — no warriors, no swords, no dragons
- **Luminous** — light is central to the story (Elder Stones glow amber/gold, the Blue Sun, the Song is described as love made audible)
- **Elevated and quiet** — closer to the covers of Guy Gavriel Kay, Madeline Miller, or Susanna Clarke than Brandon Sanderson or Joe Abercrombie
- **Emotional weight** — the cover should make you feel something, not just show you something

### Key Visual Concepts (pick one or combine)

**Option A: The Figure in the Clouds**
A solitary figure (seen from behind or in silhouette) standing on a floating stone formation high above a cloud layer. Below the clouds: darkness, the suggestion of a dead landscape. Above: a sky with a single point of golden light (the Great Elder Stone). The figure is small against the vastness — emphasizing the scale of the world and the vulnerability of the protagonist.

**Option B: The Song Made Visible**
An abstract or semi-abstract cover. Lines of golden light radiating outward from a central point (or from a figure's chest/mouth), the light traveling through layers — through stone, through cloud, through darkness. The light is warm gold at the center, fading to grey at the edges. Suggests the Song spreading through the world. Elegant, striking, literary.

**Option C: The Knife's Box**
A wooden box, open, sitting on dark stone. Inside: five objects — a blue feather, a river stone, a piece of sea glass, a dried flower, a scrap of cloth. Some glow faintly. Some are dark/blank. The simplicity of the image contrasts with its emotional weight. This is a more literary, object-focused cover — unusual for fantasy, which makes it stand out.

**Option D: The Convergence Point**
A landscape divided into zones — forest (green, dense, whispering), underground crystal (golden veins), ocean (dark, restless), desert (amber, dry), floating cloud formations (pale, luminous), volcano (red/dark), and at the center: a dead grey zone (the Murkr) with a single castle. The zones converge on the center. Suggests the scope of the world without being a traditional fantasy map.

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Gold / warm amber (the Elder Stones, the Song, magic)
- **Secondary:** Deep grey / charcoal (the Murkr, the dead zone, Varas's throne)
- **Accent:** Blue (the Blue Sun, the Saddle Bird feather, the sky)
- **Avoid:** Bright red, neon, anything that reads as "action thriller" or "urban fantasy"

### Typography
- Title should be **prominent and elegant** — literary serif font, not blocky/aggressive
- "Man Amongst the Clouds" is a long title — consider stacking: MAN / AMONGST / THE CLOUDS
- Author name: clean, smaller, positioned at bottom
- Subtitle: none
- "A Novel" optional beneath title

### Comp Title Covers to Reference
Look at the covers of:
- *Tigana* by Guy Gavriel Kay (the newer editions — atmospheric, painterly)
- *The Name of the Wind* by Patrick Rothfuss (figure, wind, atmosphere)
- *Piranesi* by Susanna Clarke (elegant, mysterious, literary)
- *The Song of Achilles* by Madeline Miller (emotional, clean, luminous)
- *The Priory of the Orange Tree* by Samantha Shannon (epic scale but elegant)

### Format Specifications
- **Ebook cover:** 2560 x 1600 pixels (Amazon KDP recommended), RGB, JPG or TIFF
- **Print cover (paperback):** Full wrap — front + spine + back. Trim size: 6" x 9". Spine width will depend on final page count (~400-450 pages). 300 DPI, CMYK, PDF with bleed.
- **Print cover (hardcover):** Same as paperback but with hardcover specifications per IngramSpark templates. Case laminate or dust jacket (designer's recommendation).
- **Back cover:** Space for blurb text (~150 words), barcode/ISBN area, author photo area (optional)
