# Author Bio

## Short Bio (50 words — for Amazon author page, back cover)

Justin Cronk writes literary fantasy about the things we lose and the things we keep. _Man Amongst the Clouds_ is his debut novel. He believes that magic should cost something, that villains deserve interiority, and that the best stories are the ones that make you listen harder. He lives in the United States.

## Medium Bio (100 words — for press, guest posts, interviews)

Justin Cronk is the author of _Man Amongst the Clouds_, a literary fantasy debut about memory, sacrifice, and what happens when a boy who was raised on silence discovers he can hear the world sing. His work explores the intersection of epic fantasy and literary fiction — the emotional interiority of characters navigating a world where magic is a conversation with memory and every act of power costs a piece of identity. He draws inspiration from Patrick Rothfuss, Robin Hobb, Ursula K. Le Guin, and Guy Gavriel Kay. _Man Amongst the Clouds_ is the first book in a planned series. He lives in the United States.

## Long Bio (200 words — for website, detailed press kit)

Justin Cronk started writing _Man Amongst the Clouds_ in March 2017. He finished it in March 2026. Nine years.

The book didn't live in one place. It lived in notes apps, emails to himself, texts to himself, Google Docs, Evernote, Scrivener — scattered across every device he owned, captured in bursts when the compulsion hit. He didn't write on a schedule. He wrote when the story demanded it, sometimes at 2 AM on his phone, sometimes in voice memos dictated while driving, sometimes in long, focused sessions that produced entire chapters. And then sometimes he didn't write for months.

The man who started this book in 2017 is not the man who finished it in 2026. He is grateful for the gap. The nine years gave the novel a depth — an understanding of grief, of love, of what it costs to carry something for a long time — that the 2017 version could not have had. The life he lived between the notes became the emotional foundation of the story.

_Man Amongst the Clouds_ is a literary fantasy about memory, sacrifice, and what happens when a boy raised on silence discovers he can hear the world sing. It is Justin's debut novel and the first in a planned series.

Justin lives in the United States.

## The Nine-Year Journey (for website "About" section)

This book started as a note on my phone in March 2017. A single idea: _What if magic was memory?_

I didn't know it would take nine years. I didn't know it would live in every notes app, email thread, and text-to-self I owned. I wrote when the compulsion hit — at 2 AM, in parking lots, in voice memos that autocorrected "spread" to "Spanish" and "natural borders" to "thing to Borders." I pasted research about medieval timber construction next to character notes about a man who drinks so his adopted son can sleep. I studied Chinese herb dispensaries to make a fictional healer's cabinet feel real. I researched MBTI psychopath profiles to understand a king who was born deaf to music in a world that sings.

The notes were scattered across Evernote, Google Docs, Scrivener, Apple Notes, emails to myself, texts to myself. For years, the novel existed as fragments — a scene here, a character sketch there, a magic system that kept evolving. The Dog Scene (a king grafting a man's head onto a dog's body) was one of the first things I wrote. Jalo's character — the drunk with the scarred face and the trembling hands and the staff that was secretly a weapon — was defined in a bullet-point list on March 29, 2017. Every single trait on that list made it into the final novel.

The protagonist was originally called Nim. He became Aelo — "breath of remembering" — when the magic system crystallized into something larger than I'd planned. The magic was originally elemental force. Standard fantasy. Powers and fireballs. At some point — I can't pinpoint exactly when — I realized: _magic should be a conversation, not a weapon._ The world remembers everything. Magic is the act of listening to those memories. And every act of listening costs you a piece of your own.

That idea changed everything. It turned a power system into a philosophy. It turned a fantasy novel into a story about what we lose to become what we're meant to be.

The man I was in 2017 couldn't have written this book. He had the bones — Jalo's trembling hands, Varas's emptiness, the boy who feels too much — but he didn't have the depth. Nine years of living gave me that. Nine years of grief, of love, of carrying things for a long time and learning what the carrying costs. The novel's central theme — that memory is the most precious thing we have and that sharing it is the bravest act a person can perform — is a theme I had to live before I could write.

I'm grateful for every year of the gap. The book is better for it. I am better for it.

The story is nearly complete. Part I is available now. The full novel is coming Fall 2026.

Thank you for being here at the beginning.

— Justin
