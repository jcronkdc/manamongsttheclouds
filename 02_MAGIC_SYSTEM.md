# THE MAGIC SYSTEM — Magic Is Memory

---

## The Core Principle

Magic is not energy. It is not force. It is **memory**.

The world remembers everything. Every stone remembers the mountain it was part of. Every river remembers the glacier that bore it. Every flame remembers the first fire that ever burned. The soil remembers every root that has ever passed through it. The wind remembers every cliff face it has ever broken against.

Magic is the ability to **commune** with these memories — to listen to the world's remembering, and to speak back to it. To ask the stone to remember a different shape. To remind flesh of what it was before the wound. To ask the air to remember a different arrangement.

When the gods created the world, they poured their own memories into it. The world is made of divine recollection. Magic, then, is the language the gods used to speak the world into being — and King Fletcher was the first mortal given the ability to hear it.

---

## The Seven Disciplines

Each discipline is a way of listening to a different kind of memory.

### 1. The Know
**What it is:** Listening to the memory of living things — their truth, intent, emotion, and history.
**What it looks like:** A Knower can feel whether someone is lying. A powerful Knower can read the shape of a person's past by touching them. The greatest Knowers can feel the emotional residue left in places — the sorrow that soaked into a battlefield, the joy embedded in a wedding hall.
**Cost:** Knowers absorb what they read. Every truth they pull from another person leaves a shadow in them. Overuse leads to emotional contamination — a Knower can lose track of which feelings are theirs.
**Limit:** You cannot Know someone whose will is stronger than yours. You cannot Know the dead (their memory has returned to the world).

### 2. The Mold
**What it is:** Speaking to stone, earth, metal, and wood — asking it to remember a different shape.
**What it looks like:** A Molder can reshape a wall into a doorway, coax iron into a blade without a forge, raise pillars from bedrock. The greatest Molders built the underground cities of the Core.
**Cost:** Every act of Molding costs a physical memory — the feeling of your own body. Molders slowly lose sensation. Their fingers go numb first, then their hands, then their arms. Old Molders can't feel rain on their skin or heat from a fire.
**Limit:** You cannot Mold living flesh (that requires Heal). You cannot Mold what you cannot touch or see. The larger the change, the more the material resists — stone that has been a mountain for a thousand years does not want to become a wall.

### 3. The Heal
**What it is:** Reminding flesh and bone of wholeness — what it was before the wound.
**What it looks like:** A Healer places hands on the wounded and listens to the body's memory of health. The flesh remembers what it was, and the Healer coaxes it back. Bones knit. Skin closes. Poison is remembered as foreign and expelled.
**Cost:** Healers take the memory of the wound into themselves. Not the wound itself — the *memory* of the pain. A Healer who mends a shattered leg will carry the ghost of that agony. Over time, Healers accumulate a library of phantom suffering. This is why many become reclusive, or drink.
**Limit:** You cannot Heal death. Once the body's memory has fully dispersed (usually within minutes of dying), the flesh forgets what it was and cannot be reminded. You cannot Heal yourself — you cannot be both the listener and the speaker. This is the cruelest rule of Healing.
**Connection to Jalo:** This is why Jalo heals everyone but himself. It's not stubbornness — *it's the law of magic.* And it's why he drinks. He carries every wound he's ever healed as a ghost in his bones.

### 4. The Move
**What it is:** Asking space and air to remember a different arrangement of things within it.
**What it looks like:** Objects lifted, hurled, rearranged. A skilled Mover can redirect arrows in flight, collapse structures from a distance, or hold a person immobile by asking the air around them to remember being solid.
**Cost:** Movers lose spatial memory — their sense of where they are in the world. Overuse leads to episodes of disorientation, vertigo, and eventually the inability to navigate familiar places. Old Movers get lost in their own homes.
**Limit:** You cannot Move something you cannot perceive. Weight matters — moving a pebble is trivial, moving a boulder costs dearly. You cannot Move magic itself.

### 5. The Guide
**What it is:** Listening to the memory of paths — where things have been and where they are going. The echo of trajectories.
**What it looks like:** A Guide always knows which direction they face. A skilled Guide can track a person across a continent by following the memory of their passage. The greatest Guides can feel the trajectory of the future — not prophecy, but the world's momentum. They sense what is *likely* the way a river senses a waterfall ahead.
**Cost:** Guides lose their memory of home. They can find anything in the world except where they came from. The more they listen to the world's paths, the more they forget their own. Old Guides are wanderers who can lead armies across deserts but cannot remember their mother's village.
**Limit:** The future is not fixed — Guide-sight becomes less reliable the further ahead it reaches. Deliberate unpredictability (chaos, madness, free will) clouds Guide-sight entirely.

### 6. The Burn
**What it is:** Speaking to fire and heat — awakening the world's memory of the first flame that burned at creation.
**What it looks like:** Flame summoned from nothing. Temperatures raised or lowered (cold is the memory of fire's absence). The Volcano People's thick skin is a generational adaptation to centuries of Burn practitioners — their bodies remember heat as home.
**Cost:** Burners lose emotional warmth. They become cold inside — detached, analytical, unable to feel joy or love or grief with the same intensity. They can summon a wall of fire but cannot feel the warmth of an embrace. This is the loneliest magic.
**Limit:** Fire is hungry and remembers being fed. It is the hardest element to control — it always wants MORE. Burners who lose concentration are consumed by their own flame. Water is fire's oldest enemy; it remembers extinguishing the first flame, and that memory is powerful.

### 7. The Sing (The Rarest)
**What it is:** Speaking to ALL memory at once. Harmony. Balance. The music the world makes when every memory resonates together. This is creation magic — the power the gods used to build reality.
**What it looks like:** No one alive has seen it in centuries. Legend says King Fletcher could Sing — and that the Elder Stone is a crystallized Song, a single perfect note frozen in time.
**Cost:** Everything. To Sing is to pour your entire self into the world's memory. You don't lose specific memories — you lose *yourself*. You become part of the world's remembering. King Fletcher Sang the Elder Stone into existence and ceased to exist as a person. He IS the stone now.
**Limit:** Only those of Fletcher's bloodline can hear the Song. This is the prophecy's meaning — not just "royal blood" but blood that *remembers the Song.*
**Connection to the protagonist:** His empathy — feeling others' pain, feeling Jalo's nightmares — isn't a side effect. It's the faintest echo of the Sing. He's hearing the world's memory without knowing what he's listening to.

## Stolen Magic — The Drain

King Varas discovered that Elder Stones could be used in reverse. Instead of storing memory willingly (as Fletcher did), you could *extract* it — ripping memory from a living person and pouring it into the stone, then consuming it yourself.

### How it works:
- The victim is connected to an Elder Stone through Blood Vine (a parasitic plant that latches onto the nervous system and acts as a conduit).
- The stone pulls memory from the victim — their magic, their experiences, their identity.
- The practitioner then draws from the stone, using the stolen memory as fuel.

### Why it's weaker:
- Stolen memory is **corrupted**. A memory given freely retains its shape and power. A memory ripped away is damaged — like a page torn from a book. You get the words but not the meaning.
- Each transfer degrades the memory further. Varas is running on copies of copies. This is why he needs an ever-growing supply.
- Corrupted memory also corrupts the user. Varas's physical transformation — his change in size, form, and color — is the result of centuries of consuming shattered memories. He's a collage of stolen lives, and his body shows it.

### Why Varas does it anyway:
- It costs him NOTHING of himself. He spends other people's memories instead of his own. In a system where magic demands sacrifice, he has found a cheat code — and it's destroying him from the inside, slowly, in ways he refuses to see.
- He is addicted. The first drain was intoxicating. Each subsequent one gives less. He chases the feeling of that first hit, but it will never come again.

## The Elder Stones

### What they are:
Ancient stones that exist at the intersection of all seven disciplines. They are the world's oldest memories crystallized into physical form. Most were created naturally over millions of years. A few were created by Fletcher through the Sing.

### Properties:
- They can **store** memory willingly given (the original purpose)
- They can **extract** memory forcibly (Varas's perversion)
- They amplify magical ability when in contact with a practitioner
- They resonate with Fletcher's bloodline — literally hum in their presence

### The Great Elder Stone:
The one Fletcher poured himself into. The most powerful object in the world. Contains the complete memory of the Sing — the music of creation itself. Hidden. Lost. The prophecy says the one of Fletcher's blood will find it and have the ability to hear its Song.

---

## Blood Vine

A parasitic plant that grows only in darkness and feeds on magical residue. In nature, it attaches to Elder Stones and slowly siphons ambient memory. Varas weaponized it — using it as a conduit between living people and Elder Stones, creating a pipeline for forced extraction.

### Properties:
- Latches onto the nervous system through the skin
- Creates a bridge between the victim's memory and the Elder Stone
- The victim feels the extraction as the loss of self — they forget things in real-time, starting with recent memories and working backward
- Prolonged exposure leaves the victim in a vegetative state — alive but empty, a body with no one inside

### In the Knower Ceremony:
Blood Vine is wrapped around children placed on Elder Stones. Under the old ways, it was used gently — a thin strand, briefly applied, enough to read a child's magical potential without harm. Under Varas, thick coils are used. Children are drained to varying degrees, some completely.

## Rules That Cannot Be Broken

1. **You cannot Heal yourself.** You cannot be both the listener and the speaker.
2. **You cannot raise the dead.** Once memory disperses fully, it returns to the world and cannot be called back to a single body.
3. **Magic always costs memory.** There are no exceptions. Even stolen magic costs the victim's memory.
4. **The Sing requires total sacrifice.** No one has ever Sung and remained themselves.
5. **The world resists being lied to.** You can ask stone to remember a different shape, but you cannot ask it to remember being water. Memory can be reshaped, not fabricated.
6. **Stolen magic degrades.** It will never be as strong as pure magic, and it always demands more.
7. **Blood cannot be hidden from blood.** Fletcher's descendants will always resonate with the Elder Stones. This is how Varas hunts them — and how the protagonist is ultimately found.

## How This Connects to Theme

- **Varas** takes and never gives. He has never sacrificed a single memory of his own. He is the richest man in the world and the emptiest. He is proof that power without sacrifice is hollow.
- **The protagonist** feels everything. He absorbs others' pain, carries their memories, loves with a depth that terrifies him. He is the opposite of Varas — all sacrifice, no hoarding. This is why he can Sing. Not because of his blood, but because of his heart.
- **Jalo** carries a lifetime of phantom wounds and grief. He sacrificed his face, his mobility, his sobriety, his identity — all to protect one child. He is the cost of love made physical.
- **The Knife** is discovering that his memories are being stolen — that even his father's love (if it ever existed) was a drain. His arc is learning what it means to CHOOSE to give instead of having it taken.
