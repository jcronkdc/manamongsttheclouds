Hi,

My name is Justin Cronk. I've been a fan of Jeff's for a long time — I listen to pretty much everything he narrates. I was actually several books into Dungeon Crawler Carl before I realized it was all one guy, and that's kind of when I got hooked on his work specifically. I probably wouldn't have picked up that series otherwise because it was hard for me to read, but listening to it I became obsessed and told everyone I knew about it.

So when I finished writing my own book and started thinking about what I'd want it to sound like as an audiobook, Jeff was the voice I kept hearing in my head. That's why I'm reaching out to you guys.

The book is called _Man Amongst the Clouds_. It's literary fantasy, about 157,000 words. The magic system is built on memory — every act of power costs the user a piece of who they are. There's a lot of emotional range and a lot of distinct character voices. The climax is a conversation, not a battle.

This is my first book ever. It's complete, though I'm still doing some editing. I have Part One up for free right now at manamongsttheclouds.com — I'm releasing it in parts while I figure out the publishing side of things. This whole process is pretty new to me, so I'm not exactly sure where to start, but I know audiobook production is something I want to explore.

I've also got a few other projects I'm chipping away at — one of them is a LitRPG, though it's on the more serious side. I wanted to write something in that space that's extremely entertaining but also has some real weight to it. More of a literary LitRPG if that makes sense. There's a sci-fi as well, and another one that's basically a retelling of the story of Jesus called _The Gospel According to a Madman_, which I love even though there's a decent chance it'll piss some people off. So I've got a lot in the works. Right now I work full-time in construction and I've been burning the candle at both ends for a few years trying to make this dream real.

Happy to send the full manuscript, a synopsis, or sample chapters — whatever works best for your evaluation.

Thanks for reading this.
