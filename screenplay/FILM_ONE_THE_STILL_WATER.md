## PROLOGUE

---

**EXT. WILDFLOWER FIELD — CONVERGENCE POINT — DAY (300 YEARS AGO)**

A field of wildflowers stretching to every horizon. Seven colors of light converge overhead — the boundaries of seven regions meeting at a single point.

KING FLETCHER (50s, weathered, kind-faced) stands barefoot in the flowers. Eyes closed. Lips parted.

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
Before there were kings, before there were borders drawn in blood and magic hoarded like grain before winter, the world sang to itself.

The flowers vibrate. Petals lift. Soil trembles. Fletcher opens his mouth. A SINGLE NOTE emerges — building, layering, harmonizing with itself. The ground crystallizes. A PILLAR OF CRYSTAL rises from the earth toward the sky.

Memories pour from Fletcher visibly — images in light: a woman's face, children laughing, bread breaking, rain falling.

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
He poured himself into it. All of him. Every memory he had. He did not lose them. He _gave_ them.

The pillar RISES through the clouds. Fletcher dissolves — not dying but dispersing, becoming part of the air, the soil, the flowers. His last expression: a smile.

**SMASH CUT TO:**

**INT. CLOUD PALACE — NURSERY — NIGHT (15 YEARS AGO)**

FIRE. Smoke. A DOOR SPLINTERING.

MAERA (30s, dark-haired, fierce, luminous) stands in a nursery doorway. Behind her: a CRIB with a BABY.

Maera SINGS. The air between her and the soldiers becomes SOLID — a wall of sound, visible as a shimmer. The soldiers FREEZE. Their faces soften. Each one looks like a child remembering something.

HALVAR ELD (30s, broad-jawed, grey-green eyes, fresh wound across his nose) in Vael Guard armor lifts the baby from the crib.

**MAERA**
_(singing between words, the note never breaking)_
Half a pinch. Every morning. Promise me.

She presses a POUCH OF HERBS into his hand. Their eyes meet. Everything passes in a look.

Halvar runs. Over a garden wall. The nursery COLLAPSES in flame behind him. Maera's note THINS. Her body becomes translucent — memories pouring out. The last thing she holds: the baby's face.

The note BREAKS. She falls.

**EXT. CANOPY FOREST — CONTINUOUS**

Halvar runs through dark woods, baby clutched to his chest. Shattered knee — the gait already uneven. Behind him: the burning palace.

The baby SCREAMS. Then stops. One breath. Small. New. The air SHIMMERS. The trees lean in. The world notices.

**HALVAR**
_(a whisper)_
Aelo.

He runs. Darkness swallows them.

**TITLE CARD:**

# MAN AMONGST THE CLOUDS

# THE STILL WATER

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
The world sang to itself, as it always had. And in a forgotten village at the edge of the Canopy, fifteen years later, a boy woke before dawn because the man who raised him was having nightmares again.

---

## ACT ONE: THE SILENCE

---

**INT. COTTAGE — AELO'S ROOM — PRE-DAWN**

Darkness. The sound of THRASHING through a thin wall. A bottle rolling.

AELO (15, thin, dark-haired, too small for his age) lies in bed, eyes open. He presses the heels of his palms into his eyes. Through the wall: a WOMAN'S VOICE — high, clear, holding a single note. Then: silence. Sudden. Total.

Aelo exhales.

---

**INT. COTTAGE — MAIN ROOM — DAWN**

A small stone cottage. Herb jars line every shelf. Snares and skinning knives on the walls.

Aelo moves through a ROUTINE: three jars for Jalo's tincture — measured, mixed, set at Jalo's place.

Then: the FOURTH JAR. Highest shelf. Unmarked. Clay. Black wax seal. He spoons the contents into his porridge. The world goes QUIETER — as if someone placed a hand over a bell.

JALO (60s, half his face wrapped in stained linen, cane — TAP-STEP-DRAG) appears in the doorway. Sits. Drinks the tincture. Watches Aelo eat.

CLOSE ON: Jalo's eyes tracking the spoon. The swallow. Then — the EXHALE. Fractional. Shoulders dropping. As if the most important thing in his day has been accomplished.

**AELO**
Bad night?

**JALO**
Wind.

There was no wind.

---

**EXT. SETTLEMENT 34 — DAY**

A village too small to have a proper name. Forty families. A mill. A smokehouse.

---

**INT. SCHOOLHOUSE — DAY**

HALLA (40s, braid so tight it pulls her face) lectures before a painted board: a smiling king on a golden throne, happy children.

**HALLA**
In the age before the Unification, the regions were fractured. Lawless. Magic was used recklessly—

Aelo sits at his desk. He's not listening to the words. He's feeling her HEARTBEAT — a faint rhythmic disturbance in the air between them. Every time Halla says _lawless_, _safely managed_, _compassionate system_ — the heartbeat QUICKENS. A flinch.

CLOSE ON: Aelo's face. Reading the distance between what she says and what her body means. Halla is afraid of the story she's telling.

---

**EXT. CANOPY FOREST — AFTERNOON**

Aelo walks home through massive trees. He finds a SMALL BIRD at the base of an elm. A Hopper. Wing shattered.

He cups it in his hands. Its heartbeat — frantic, tiny — against his palms. He WANTS to fix it. A heat in his palms. A pressure in his chest. As if there's a door and if he could just find the handle—

The heartbeat stops.

He feels something LEAVE. A dispersal. The bird's weight unchanged, but something departing — spreading into the roots, the soil, the air. The bird's memory, going home.

Aelo cries. Quietly. Not for the bird. For himself. For the thing he almost was.

---

**INT. COTTAGE — EVENING**

Jalo prepares the next morning's porridge. Aelo watches.

**AELO**
A bird died today. In my hands.

Jalo's hands stop. Half a second.

**JALO**
That happens.

**AELO**
I felt it go. Not die. _Go._ Like something leaving.

Three full breaths.

**JALO**
You have a gentle heart. Gentle hearts feel things that harder hearts don't.

**AELO**
Is that why I feel your nightmares?

Jalo turns. Beneath his composure — something vast straining against his ribs.

**JALO**
Go to sleep, Aelo.

---

**INT. COTTAGE — AELO'S ROOM — NIGHT**

Aelo lies in the dark. Between waking and sleep: a HUM. Low, vast, barely perceptible. As if the walls, the soil, the roots, the stone of the hearth — everything is making a sound together.

The herbs pull it under. He sleeps.

---

**INT. CASTLE — BAARA'S QUARTERS — PRE-DAWN**

A stone cell. A cot. No mirror. Beneath the cot: a WOODEN BOX.

THE KNIFE — BAARA (35, lean, precise, eyes that assess everything and feel nothing) — touches the box with his foot. Confirming.

He dresses. Dark armor. A STONE goes into his belt — smooth, round, heavy.

---

**INT. CASTLE — THE CHAMBER — MORNING**

The Knife descends. The walls PULSE with reddish light — Blood Vine threaded through the stone.

The Chamber: FORTY-SEVEN PEOPLE in alcoves. Connected to Elder Stones by Blood Vine entering their skin at the skull. Eyes open. Staring at nothing.

The Knife walks the rows with a leather log. Checking drain rates. Recording. He does not look at their eyes.

He pauses at ROW 4, ALCOVE 6. An ancient MING WOMAN. Supposed to be comatose. Her eyes are TRACKING HIM. Her lips move — no sound, but the shape unmistakable: she is trying to speak.

He records: _Elevated awareness. Motor function. Monitor._

At the back of the Chamber: a SEALED STONE DOOR. No handle. The stone is WARM. Behind it — the faintest sound of BREATHING. Words on a loop, at a volume below hearing.

The Knife presses his palm to the door. Warm. Like skin. He pulls away. Does not record this.

---

**INT. CASTLE — THRONE ROOM — DAY**

Seven flights of stairs. No lifts. By design.

KING VARAS on an OBSIDIAN THRONE. Massive. Dense. Bark-textured skin, swollen hands, flat amber eyes. Blood Vine runs from the armrests into his forearms. He does not look up.

**VARAS**
Report.

The Knife reports. Then:

**VARAS**
The boy.

Beat.

**VARAS (CONT'D)**
A routine survey. A calibration stone. A child walked past. The stone hummed.

**THE KNIFE**
Hummed.

**VARAS**
I want the child found. Bring the child if it's nothing. Bring the child's head if it's something.

Beat.

**VARAS (CONT'D)**
Elder Stones don't sing, Baara. They haven't sung in three hundred years. Whatever that child is, I want to hear what makes a stone remember its voice.

**THE KNIFE**
Yes, my lord.

He turns to leave.

**VARAS**
Baara.

The muscles in The Knife's back tighten.

**VARAS (CONT'D)**
Be efficient.

Not _be careful_. Not _come home safely_.

**THE KNIFE**
Always, my lord.

---

**INT. CASTLE CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS**

The Knife stops where no one can see him. From inside his coat: a BLUE FEATHER. Small. Iridescent. Blue shifting toward green.

He turns it in his fingers. Reaches for the memory — a bird, the Canopy, he'd been young. The details are FOG. The bird had been — the word _beautiful_ supplies itself, but the FEELING behind it is gone.

He holds it for ELEVEN SECONDS. Counted against his pulse. Then puts it back. Straightens. Walks away.

---

**EXT. SETTLEMENT 34 — THREE DAYS LATER**

The village is changing. MOTHERS stop laughing. They watch their children walk to school and do not go inside until the children are out of sight. The baker puts extra sugar in the rolls and doesn't charge.

---

**INT. COTTAGE — NIGHT**

Through the crack in his door, Aelo watches Jalo UNWRAP HIS FACE.

Layer by layer. The linen reveals: a LANDSCAPE of damage. Ridged, cratered, skin pulled tight in some places and hanging loose in others. The brow melted. The eye socket reshaped by fire.

Not an accident. An ERASURE. Someone decided this face needed to stop being recognizable.

---

**FLASHBACK — EXT. CANOPY RIVER — NIGHT (15 YEARS AGO)**

A river in the deep Canopy. Cold. Clear. HALVAR (younger, face whole, the baby in a sling on his chest) kneels at the bank. He looks at his REFLECTION. The face of a dead man — not dead yet, but dead soon if the face remains. Halvar Eld. Captain of the Vael Guard. Every Mrak Patrol in the kingdom carries a description.

He heats a SKINNING KNIFE over a fire of Canopy deadwood. The blade turns red. Then white at the tip.

The baby is sleeping. The herbs held. The silence is total.

He does not hesitate. He presses the flat of the blade to his left cheek and HOLDS IT THERE.

The sound: not a hiss. A POP. Deep, subcutaneous. He bites through the leather strap between his teeth. He does not drop the blade. He moves it — methodically, across the cheek, up to the brow, around the eye socket _(careful, the eye is the one thing he cannot lose)_, down to the jaw.

Then he plunges his face into the river. The cold is a mercy so sharp it feels like a second burning. The water turns pink.

Halvar Eld died in that river. Jalo Soturi was born in the same water, screaming.

---

**BACK TO PRESENT — INT. COTTAGE — NIGHT**

Jalo reaches for his cane — and in the candlelight, it's not a cane. It's a STAFF. Longer, thicker, carved with markings that GLOW AMBER.

He lifts his wrist to the candle. A MARK — amber, angular, like a letter from an outlawed alphabet. The same mark Aelo has on his own wrist.

Aelo's mark PULSES in answer. A bell rung in another room.

Jalo presses his forehead to the staff. His shoulders fold. A sound quieter than crying — the mechanism worn down to essentials.

---

**EXT. CEREMONY SQUARE — DAWN**

Twelve soldiers arrive. Mrak Patrol. Dark armor. Diamond-tipped spears. A CAPTAIN with a narwhal-horn crest.

An ELDER STONE on a wooden platform. BLOOD VINE draped across it — pulsing, alive. Waiting to be fed.

The KNOWER beside it: gaunt, trembling, eyes emptied.

Children line up. One by one, the Knower presses his palm to each forehead. The Stone glows dimly for minor talents.

A RED-HAIRED GIRL (14) steps up. The Stone BLAZES green. The captain grips her arm.

**CAPTAIN**
This one registers. She comes with us.

Her mother SCREAMS — the raw animal sound of a parent whose child is being taken. A soldier twists the mother's arm behind her back. The girl is led away. She looks back once. No one stops them.

The crowd stands and watches. The silence is the loudest thing Aelo has ever heard.

AELO'S TURN. He steps onto the platform. Bare feet. The Knower's palm — cold, trembling — presses his forehead.

The Elder Stone SINGS.

Not a hum. A SOUND. Clear, high, sustained. LIGHT pours from the Stone — not any color. Light itself. The Blood Vine RECOILS from Aelo's skin as if burned.

Every soldier in the square feels the first memory they ever made. For THREE SECONDS, every man is a child again. The cruelty they learned — a thin veneer over the wonder they were born with.

Three seconds. Then the Knower GRABS Aelo's shoulders. His empty eyes suddenly, impossibly full:

**KNOWER**
Run.

The soldiers recover. Spears lower. The crowd breaks.

Jalo is already moving. The cane COMES APART — a twist, a pull — the STAFF inside blazing amber. He plants his bad foot and STRIKES THE GROUND.

The earth OPENS. Six feet of stone surge upward. Two soldiers caught mid-lunge disappear beneath the surge. The sound is a groan — deep, tectonic. Dust billows. Mold magic — raw, fifteen years of grief behind the first stroke.

Jalo's hand — iron grip — on Aelo's arm. Both eyes open, scarred and clear. His face is CALM. The real calm. The calm of a man who has been waiting for this.

**JALO**
With me. Now.

They RUN. Through the village. Into the tree line. The canopy closes overhead. Branches PULL BACK. Roots FLATTEN. The forest is making room.

The Elder Stone: STILL SINGING. The note sounds like a woman's voice. Like the voice from Jalo's nightmares. Like someone who has been waiting a very long time to say his name.

---

## ACT TWO: THE RUNNING

---

**EXT. DEEP CANOPY — CONTINUOUS**

Trees WHISPERING. Not wind. Voices. Dozens. Hundreds.

The herbs are FAILING — burned off by the running, the terror, the Stone. The bell is RINGING — a real frequency in his sternum, his teeth.

**AELO**
Jalo — the trees are—

**JALO**
I know. _Run._

The forest carries them. Roots soften. Branches swing clear. Behind them: armor, boots, and a SNUFFLING — rapid, percussive. An animal.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
They have a Rat.

Jalo's knee GIVES. A wet mechanical failure. He goes down HARD. The staff flies from his hand — his fingers opened too easily. The Molding at the square cost him sensation.

**AELO**
I'm not leaving you.

The words come from the bell-place. Solid. Dense.

Jalo looks up. Something broken open in his face.

**JALO**
You sound like her.

**AELO**
Like who?

---

**EXT. HOLLOW IN A CANOPY ELM — NIGHT**

Amber staff-light. Jalo's face — BOTH sides — visible for the first time. The scars are worse in this light.

**JALO**
Your name is Aelo. That much is true. I gave it to you the night I took you from your mother.

Beat.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
From a burning room in a palace above the Clouds. From a woman who was singing your name while the door came down.

**AELO**
Took me from where?

**JALO**
My name is not Jalo Soturi. It was Halvar Eld. I was Captain of the Vael Guard. We served King Aldric. Your father.

**AELO**
The Cloud Kingdom doesn't exist—

**JALO**
The history is a painting on a board told by a woman whose heart flinches every time she speaks. The Cloud Kingdom existed. Your mother was its greatest Singer. And King Varas destroyed it because your bloodline carries something he cannot steal.

**AELO**
What?

**JALO**
The Sing.

The word lands. Settles into Aelo with the ease of a key entering a lock.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
The herbs I've been giving you suppress your magic. Completely. Since you were an infant. Your mother asked me to protect you. The only way to hide you was to silence the song in your blood.

**AELO**
You drugged me. Every morning. My entire life.

**JALO**
_(raw)_
Kept you alive. Yes. Every morning. Your mother asked me. I promised.

---

**EXT. HOLLOW — DAWN**

Aelo has not slept. The whispering has EVOLVED — he can almost catch words.

Jalo reaches for the HERB JAR.

**JALO**
You need to—

**AELO**
No.

One syllable. Fifteen years of swallowed questions.

**AELO (CONT'D)**
I can hear them. The trees. I am not putting that jar in my mouth again.

**JALO**
Without the herbs, your signature—

**AELO**
The stone _sang_, Jalo. They know where to look. The herbs won't change that.

Jalo takes out the jar. Holds it. Sets it on the ground. Does not open it.

**JALO**
What do you hear?

Aelo closes his eyes. Releases.

The world POURS IN. Visual representation: the camera PULLS OUTWARD from Aelo's face — through the hollow, through the elm's roots, through the surrounding forest — showing us the web of living memory, the trees and the soil and the water all VIBRATING, all speaking, all connected.

**AELO**
_(face wet)_
Everything. I hear everything.

Jalo's hand on his shoulder. Both sides of his ruined face hold one expression: a man watching the thing he protected become what it was always meant to be.

**JALO**
Then we'd better teach you how to listen.

---

**EXT. TRADE ROAD — DAY**

SERETH sits on a rock, peeling an apple. They appear as a MIDDLE-AGED MERCHANT — weathered, one-eyed.

They bite into the apple. Close their eyes. Juice runs down their chin. Then the Elder Stone's note reaches them from forty miles away.

Sereth DROPS THE APPLE. Their hands shake — with RESONANCE. They know this sound. They heard it three hundred years ago when Fletcher sang.

They stand. The apple lies in the dirt. One bite missing. Already browning. They have been leaving things behind for three hundred years — possessions, identities, the faces of people they loved.

They think of ESSA. They always think of Essa. A Guide. Sea-born. Dark-skinned. Hands that moved through water the way Sereth's voice had once moved through the divine register. She could hold a bowl of seawater and ask it to remember being rain, and the salt would separate from the fresh. She had done this for Sereth once, moonlight on her shoulders, and Sereth had thought: _This. This is why I gave them the Song._

She had died. Eighty-one years — the blink of a divine eye. Sereth had felt her go. A silence where a voice had been.

Three hundred years of penance. Every drained prisoner, every corrupted ceremony, every child taken — all of it traced back to a night by a fire when a god said to a kind man: _Would you like to hear the world sing?_

They close their eyes and CHANGE. The merchant dissolves. An OLD CANOPY WOMAN emerges — broad-shouldered, grey-haired, calloused hands, unremarkable. Forgettable. Safe.

---

**EXT. TRADE ROAD — LATER**

Six Mrak soldiers. Sereth — now an OLD CANOPY WOMAN — steps aside deferentially.

**CAPTAIN**
You see anyone? A man and a boy. The man limps.

**SERETH**
_(perfect Canopy accent)_
North road, two days past. Heading toward Deeproot.

The patrol wheels north. Exactly the wrong direction. Sereth watches them go — then turns south, toward the amber glow flickering deep in the forest.

---

**EXT. HOLLOW — NIGHT**

Sereth enters the amber light. Old woman's body — but the eyes are wrong. Too deep. Too layered.

Jalo's hand goes to the staff.

**JALO**
Who are you?

Sereth looks at Aelo — sleeping, twitching, murmuring in a language not human. Then at Jalo:

**SERETH**
I am the reason you burned your face. And I am very sorry.

Jalo feels the edges of something too large to fit in the world's memory.

**JALO**
You're the one who started all of this. Three hundred years—

**SERETH**
If I had touched the bloodline directly, the other six would have found you. My distance was the only protection—

**JALO**
_Protection?_ I burned my own face. I drugged a child every morning for fifteen years—

**SERETH**
Yes. And it was not enough. And the boy needs more than a wall between him and the fire. He needs to learn to Sing.

**JALO**
Can you teach him?

**SERETH**
The Sing cannot be taught. It can only be remembered. But I can take you to the people who can help him remember. One discipline at a time. Each one a piece of the Song.

---

**EXT. CANOPY CLEARING — MORNING**

Aelo wakes SCREAMING. His body has CHANGED overnight — taller, shoulders broader. The herbs' suppression releasing.

The full voice of the Canopy at dawn — every tree, every root, every insect, every molecule of water — hits him. The sound comes through his SKIN.

The scream has HARMONICS. It hits the elm and the elm VIBRATES. Birds DETONATE from the canopy. Deer crash through undergrowth. Every insect in a half-mile goes SILENT simultaneously.

Three seconds. Then silence.

Aelo on his hands and knees. Tears. Blood from his lip.

**SERETH**
That was necessary. The compression had to release.

Jalo kneels before Aelo:

**JALO**
What you're hearing — all of it — it is not many things. It is one thing. The world's memory is one memory. It just has many voices.

He places his hand on Aelo's chest.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
Listen for the space between the voices. That silence is _you_.

Aelo closes his eyes. First attempt: nothing. Second: a FLICKER — two voices pause at different moments. A sliver of silence. Third: he listens THROUGH the voices. Not at the glass — through it.

He finds it. A POCKET. Small. Fragile. Entirely his.

**AELO**
I found it.

**JALO**
Hold it.

**AELO**
For how long?

**JALO**
_(hand on his chest, eyes wet)_
For the rest of your life.

---

**THE REMEMBERING**

**EXT. CANOPY — THE REMEMBERING WALL — DUSK**

A wall — not built but GROWN. Canopy elms, hundreds of them, trunks fused over centuries into a continuous surface fifty feet high. The oldest living structure in the Canopy.

Jalo places his palm on the wall. Speaks in an OLD LANGUAGE. The wall REMEMBERS him. A gate APPEARS — the fused trunks separating.

Then he does something he has never done. He KNEELS.

His bad knee screams — Aelo can feel it through the earth. But Jalo kneels in the gate of the Remembering with the sky behind him.

**JALO**
Everything I've done. Every lie. Every herb. Every night I drank instead of holding you. It was for this moment.

Beat.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
I would do it all again. Every miserable second of it. The blade. The herbs. The bottle. The lies. I would carry you out of that palace and disappear into this forest and spend another fifteen years watching you eat the thing that keeps you small, if the alternative was a world without you in it.

His forehead presses to his folded hands. His entire body trembles.

**JALO (CONT'D)**
That's not an apology. That's worse than an apology. That's the truth.

Aelo walks to him. Kneels across from him. Puts his hand on Jalo's shoulder.

**AELO**
I know.

Two words. Not enough. But truest he has.

They cross the threshold together.

---

## ACT THREE: THE LEARNING

---

**INTERCUT: THE KNIFE'S HUNT**

**EXT. SETTLEMENT 34 — CEREMONY SQUARE — DAY**

The Knife arrives at the village. The square swept clean. The rubble distributed. But the ELDER STONE remains — sitting on its platform. Two guards posted. The stone is still WARM.

The Knife walks to it. The square is empty — villagers confined to their homes. He can feel their eyes through the cracks. He does not care.

He places his hand on the stone.

The hum enters through his palm. Travels up his arm. Settles in his chest — in a HOLLOW he did not know was there. For half a second: WARMTH. Not temperature. _Belonging._ The feeling of being part of something. As if the hollow were a socket and the hum the missing piece.

He pulls his hand away. The warmth VANISHES. The hollow remains — but now it has EDGES. He knows its shape. He knows the dimensions of what is missing.

He stands very still. His face shows nothing. He does not report the note to his father.

This piece of information is his. The first thing that has been his in a very long time.

---

**INT. SCHOOLHOUSE — DAY**

He interrogates the KNOWER.

**KNOWER**
The stone sang. Not hummed. _Sang._ One note that was every note. I told him to run. That was the last useful thing I will ever do.

**THE KNIFE**
Why?

**KNOWER**
Because what I felt was not a discipline. It was all of them. Simultaneously. The last time that happened was Fletcher.

---

**EXT. SETTLEMENT 34 — EDGE — NIGHT**

The Knife holds a COMMUNICATION STONE — the size of a child's fist, the color of a bruise, threaded with Blood Vine. It grows warm. Then warmer. Then hot.

Varas's voice arrives not through the air but through the BONE — vibrating up the arm, settling in the skull. The King's words originate inside The Knife's own head.

**VARAS (V.O.)**
You are late.

The Knife reports: the boy, the Stone, the escape. The old man's Mold magic.

**VARAS (V.O.)**
Halvar Eld.

The name arrives with weight.

**VARAS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
Captain of the Vael Guard. Reported dead in the purge. I burned his order to ash fifteen years ago — twelve men in a stone hall, the doors sealed, the fire fed until the stone itself cracked. One body was never confirmed.

Beat.

**VARAS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
The boy made a stone sing. Stones have not sung in three hundred years. It means the bloodline survived. Fletcher's blood. In a boy. In my kingdom. Making stones remember how to speak.

The stone in The Knife's palm passes from hot to searing. He does not shift his grip.

**VARAS (V.O.) (CONT'D)**
Find the boy. Do not kill him. Do not harm him. Bring him to the castle intact. I want to hear what makes a stone sing.

The stone goes cold. The connection severs. The Knife stands in the square with the dead stone in his palm and the living stone humming twenty feet away.

He walks to the Elder Stone one more time. Places his hand on it. Briefly. A second. Less.

The warmth comes and goes. The hollow pulses. The shape of the missing thing sharpens.

He withdraws his hand. Walks to his mount. Does not look back.

---

**EXT. MRAK CAMP — NIGHT**

The Knife assembles his hunting party. A MING TRACKER — small, dark-haired, flat black eyes — sits at the perimeter, palms on the earth.

**TRACKER**
The signature isn't a trail. It's a saturation. He's soaking into everything he passes through. What I'm reading is the harmonic signature of something that hasn't existed in three hundred years.

A FLASH RAT — bred for pursuit, narrow skull, oversized nostrils — SCREAMS when released. Overwhelmed by the signal.

---

**EXT. MING BORDER — DAY**

The landscape changes. A LAKE stretches before them — vast, grey-green, impossibly still. Low stone buildings emerge from the waterline. A monastery built INTO the lake.

Aelo's knees buckle. The lake's memory — every raindrop, every fish, every season — floods in. And beneath it: a TONE from below the bedrock. A sound so deep it's less heard than inhabited.

---

**INT. MING MONASTERY — DAY**

The ELDEST PRACTITIONER — ancient, dark-skinned, flat black Ming eyes with extraordinary depth — approaches Jalo.

**ELDER**
Guardian. You carry so much pain.

**JALO**
I carry what needs carrying.

**ELDER**
You carry what you refuse to set down. The boy is here. The boy is safe. You do not have to hold it all anymore.

The silence that follows is the loudest thing Aelo has ever felt from Jalo. Then — Jalo's face COLLAPSES. Not like a wall. Like a held breath ending. He kneels. She places her palm on his temple.

Aelo watches through the Know: fifteen years of phantom suffering flowing OUTWARD. Into hands trained to hold it. Not removing — distributing.

Jalo WEEPS. Full. Structural. The sound of a foundation feeling another wall take the load. The relief so acute it expresses itself as grief.

---

**EXT. MING LAKE — DAWN — TRAINING MONTAGE**

The TEACHER — expressionless, still, face at perfect equilibrium.

**TEACHER**
Feel nothing. Find the riverbed.

Day 1: Aelo at the lake's edge. Drowning in noise.

Day 3: Trying not to try. Layers stratifying.

Day 4: Jalo sits beside him. CHANGED — rested, tremor reduced. For the first time, Aelo feels HOPE from the old man. Fragile. A cupped flame in a drafty room.

Day 5: Something inside Aelo SETTLES. He sinks. Past the pocket. Past the silence between voices. Into a depth where every voice organizes into a single structure.

At the bottom: A TONE. Clear. Warm. Impossibly old. The Elder Stone beneath the lake. The sound of sacrifice singing to itself in the dark.

Three breaths. Then his body pulls him back. He surfaces. Tears on his face.

**AELO**
What is that sound?

The teacher's face holds — for the first time — an expression. Wonder. And fear.

**TEACHER**
That is the question your blood was born to answer.

---

**INT. MING MONASTERY — OREN'S CHAMBER**

OREN (20, slight, composed) holds Aelo's hands palm to palm.

We enter Oren's mind: CLEAN CORRIDORS. Doors closed but unlocked.

First room: Oren at twelve. His MOTHER braiding his hair. Each pass of the braid a sentence in the grammar of daily love.

Second room: GRIEF. A remembering-stone by the lake. A brother's name. The grief is vast — but it's in a ROOM. Visited deliberately. Exited when visiting is done.

**OREN (V.O.)**
Not suppression. Curation. The memory exists. It doesn't have to fill every room.

Third room: A WILD GARDEN. Not organized — felt. Dozens of sunsets layered. The taste of a plum eaten at thirteen when he was grief-sick and a fellow monk pressed the fruit into his hand. The messy, overwhelming, beautiful reason you build the architecture.

**OREN (V.O.)**
The corridors are the structure. The garden is the reason you build it.

---

**INT. MING MONASTERY — WORKROOM — EVENING**

Jalo teaches Aelo to make a real tincture. Side by side. Without hiding half the lesson.

**JALO**
Breedlebuck. For inflammation. The body remembers the swelling. The herb reminds it of the time before.

**AELO**
You're not adding medicine. You're reminding.

Jalo looks at him. Surprised — not at the observation. At the boy making it.

---

**INT. MING MONASTERY — TERRACE — NIGHT**

Jalo is TELLING STORIES. Animated. Laughing. Monks rapt.

**JALO**
A Greensong runner. She came through the trees — not running. _Flowing._ The forest was drumming for her. I was in love for approximately four seconds.

He laughs. The laugh COMPLETES itself without the usual tightening. Settles into silence — not loaded, not defended. Easy.

Aelo watches. The man on the terrace is who Jalo was BEFORE. The trapper, the drunk — a construction. A shelter built to survive a storm. This is the man his mother trusted.

---

**EXT. MING LAKE — DEPARTURE**

The elder woman presents: a LAKE STONE. Small. Dark. Three centuries of water. Aelo takes it — the riverbed comes INSTANTLY.

**TEACHER**
Do not thank me. Come back and Sing.

---

**INTERCUT: THE KNIFE — CAMP — NIGHT**

The hunting party camps at the Canopy's edge. A fire. The Flash Rat whining in its kennel.

A YOUNG VOLCANO SOLDIER sits by the fire, talking to another soldier. His voice low, his face doing something The Knife recognizes but cannot produce — a softening. The muscular configuration of thinking about someone you love when they are not present.

**YOUNG SOLDIER**
Seven now. Collects shells. Has a box of them under her bed — sorted by color, if you can believe it. She knows where each one came from. This one's from the north beach. This one's from the tide pools. This one she found in a market and the merchant said it came from the deep ocean, and she carries it in her pocket every day because she says it's the farthest-away thing she owns.

The other soldier laughs. The young one continues — the daughter's habits, her questions, the way she eats breakfast one item at a time starting with the thing she likes least: "Because then the last taste in your mouth is the good one."

The Knife LISTENS. With an intensity disproportionate to the content. A starving man watching someone eat. The soldier's daughter is seven. She collects shells. She sorts them by color. She has a system for breakfast.

These are small details. Ordinary. The architecture of a life that is allowed to accumulate without someone reaching in and removing the bricks.

The soldier glances at The Knife. The glance contains what every glance directed at The Knife contains: fear, thinly disguised as deference. The story stops.

The Knife stands. Walks to his tent.

---

**INT. THE KNIFE'S TENT — NIGHT**

The box. Five objects on the cot. He reaches for each.

The cloth: BLANK. The story is gone. The woman's smile — gone.

The flower: fading. Purple dissolving. The crack in the prison floor going soft.

Four left. Three fading.

**THE KNIFE (V.O.)**
I am a countdown. And when I reach zero, I will not know I've reached it. And my father will have eaten every beautiful thing I ever found, and he will not have tasted a single one.

Outside, the FLASH RAT screams. The TRACKER — SERA — sits at the perimeter, palms on the earth. Her flat black eyes find The Knife across the fire. She holds his gaze for a moment too long — the volumetric stare of a Know-practitioner seeing deeper than surface.

She looks away. The Knife notes it. Files it. Does not understand it.

He closes the box. Holds it against his chest. Eleven seconds.

---

**EXT. CANOPY ROAD — SOUTH — DAY**

Aelo, Jalo, and Sereth walk south. The Remembering wall behind them.

Jalo limps between them — staff and knee — but LIGHTER. The pain-sharing gave him back inches of motion.

**JALO**
The Core first. Torvus is there. Then the Sea.

**AELO**
Who is Torvus?

**JALO**
A man who is very large and very loud and who owes me several drinks.

The road stretches ahead. The sky is enormous after the canopy.

Aelo walks. The lake stone in his numb left hand. The bell in his chest humming. The first discipline behind him. The second ahead.

---

**EXT. HILLTOP — OVERLOOKING THE ROAD — CONTINUOUS**

From a hilltop, the boy and the guardian look like ants. SERETH watches from a distance — the old Canopy woman, sitting on a rock, the pack on her back.

The old woman's body is wrong now — wrong for where Sereth is going. The Sea doesn't speak wood. The Sea speaks salt and tide.

They close their eyes. Breathe. Remember.

The old woman DISSOLVES — not dramatically. Like a sentence being revised. The same words, rearranged. A MIDDLE-AGED FISHERMAN emerges — lean, sun-weathered, dark skin, salt-stiffened hair. One eye — the left scarred over, a detail carried from body to body because the loss is real.

They open the new eye. The hilltop looks different through it — the colors shifted, recalibrated by the new body's optics.

**SERETH (V.O.)**
I collect orphans the way other people collect regrets. Perhaps they are the same thing.

They have a letter to deliver. Four words, composed over fifteen years: _He is worth the grief._

The fisherman turns south. The Sea is weeks away. Laine is there — Essa's descendant. She does not know about the connection. She knows only that every trajectory she follows ends in grief.

Sereth walks. Behind them, a boy holds a stone and listens to the world. And the world listens back.

The journey has begun.
