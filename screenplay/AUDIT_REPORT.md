# ERRORS

## 1. FACTUAL ERROR: The Dead Bird Listed as a Cost of the Song

**Film Three, Act Two (The Singing):**

> "Each costs a memory: porridge, the schoolteacher, the canopy at dawn, dirt, the workshop, the dead bird."

**The Problem:** The novel's Ch 40 initially lists the bird among the costs, but Ch 41 — the definitive post-Song inventory — explicitly states the bird SURVIVED:

> "The bird. The dead bird in the yard. He could still feel it — the weight of the small body in his hands..."

The bird is one of the deepest, body-stored memories that the Song could NOT reach. It's the first grief. It survives because first griefs are somatic, not mental. The bird surviving is thematically critical — it's the memory that started everything.

**Fix:** Replace "the dead bird" in the Song's cost list with another memory (e.g., "the feel of the lake stone's surface" or "the sound of Rise's wings").

---

## 2. TIMELINE ERROR: Sera the Tracker's "Mark on Your Neck" Revelation

**Film One, Act Three (Intercut: The Knife — Night):**
The screenplay places Sera's revelation ("The mark on your neck. The one you think is a birthmark.") during the Canopy pursuit in Film 1.

**The Problem:** In the novel, this happens in **Ch 27** — at the base of the mountains, AFTER the Knife has tracked Aelo through six regions over many weeks. It occurs after Sera Knows The Knife across the campfire and flinches at what she sees. The revelation is the culmination of weeks of proximity, not something that happens early in the hunt.

**Fix:** Move Sera's mark revelation to Film 2, Act Two (at the mountain base camp, before the Blue Sun night). Film 1's Knife intercuts should end instead with the cloth going blank + the "I am a countdown" V.O. — which is already there and strong enough as a cliffhanger.

---

## 3. MINOR ERROR: Maera's Marriage

**Film Two, Cael's narration:**

> "Married to Aldric's grandson."

**The Problem:** The novel has an internal inconsistency. Ch 24 says "married King Aldric's eldest grandson," but Ch 4 says "We served King Aldric. Your father." and Ch 44 has Vero calling Aldric "our father." The dominant reading throughout the novel is that Aldric is Aelo's grandfather (father of Varas and Vero), and Maera married one of Aldric's sons or into the next generation.

**Fix:** Change to "married into Aldric's line" or simply "married your father" to avoid the contradiction. This is a novel-level inconsistency, but the screenplay should be internally consistent.

# MISSING SCENES (Should Be Added)

## 4. The Knife Touches the Elder Stone — First Hollow (Ch 8)

**Missing from:** Film 1, Act Three

**What's lost:** The moment The Knife places his hand on the ceremony Stone and feels — for the first time — the hollow in his chest. Half a second of warmth, belonging, being part of something. Then he pulls his hand away and the warmth vanishes. This is the ORIGIN of his arc. Without it, his later moments (the sand, the handshake) lack their foundation.

**Recommendation:** Add a brief scene in Film 1's intercut: The Knife arrives at the village, approaches the still-warm Stone, places his hand on it. Half a second of warmth. He pulls away. The hollow has edges now. He does not report this.

## 7. The Warden Scene (Ch 45)

**Missing from:** Film 3 screenplay (listed in Trilogy Structure doc but not written)

## 8. Halvar's Face-Burning Flashback (Ch 6)

**Missing from:** All three films

**What's lost:** The most visceral scene in the novel. Halvar at a river, three days after the nursery. Looking at his reflection. Heating a skinning knife. Pressing the flat of the blade to his cheek. The sound — a pop, not a hiss. The smell — pork. The pain. "Halvar Eld died in that river. Jalo Soturi was born in the same water."

**Recommendation:** Add as a brief FLASHBACK in Film 1 — when Aelo watches Jalo unwrap his face through the door. Or in Film 2 — when Jalo tells Aelo "I burned my face." The audience needs to SEE the sacrifice, not just hear about it.

## 9. Aelo's Key Dialogue Before Confronting The Knife (Ch 29)

**Missing from:** Film 2, Act Three

**What's lost:** Aelo's reasoning for going alone — specifically the line about The Knife's box: "The Knife carries five objects. Three are blank. Two still hold light. The Knife checks them every night because the checking is the only act he performs that is not function but _choice_, and the choosing is the proof that the person is still inside the weapon." This is the thematic thesis of the confrontation.

**Recommendation:** Add this dialogue to the Film 2 scene where Aelo tells Jalo and Laine he's going alone.

# CONTINUITY / STRUCTURAL NOTES

## 15. The Knife's Box Object Status Tracking

The five objects fade in a specific order across the novel:

1. **Cloth** → blank in Ch 12 (during Canopy hunt)
2. **Flower** → blank in Ch 22 (at the coast, same night as Aelo's "we can't keep running")
3. **River stone** → blank in Ch 27 (at the mountain base)
4. **Feather** → fading throughout, still present at death
5. **Sea glass** → fading throughout, still present at death

The screenplays compress this into approximate beats but should track it precisely. Currently Film 1 shows the cloth blank. Film 2's structure doc says "flower and stone go blank" but the screenplay doesn't explicitly show each going blank. Each blanking should be a SCENE — a brief, gut-punch intercut of The Knife reaching for an object and finding nothing.

## 16. Aelo's Numbness Progression

The novel tracks this precisely:

- **Hands** (fingertips to wrists): from the sealed passage in the Core (Ch 18)
- **Forearms**: from the sealed passage continuation
- **Elbows**: permanent by the time of the Clouds

The screenplays mention the numbness but don't always track the progression. The first cost (feet going dim during the brick exercise, Ch 18) is omitted — the screenplay jumps straight to the sealed passage's major cost.

## 17. Sereth's Body Changes

Novel: Merchant (male) → Old Canopy woman → Fisherman (male) → True form
Screenplay: Merchant → Old Canopy woman → (implied fisherman, not shown changing) → True form
The fisherman transition is mentioned in the novel's Ch 15 and referenced in Film 2 (Laine's "one-eyed fisherman" letter) but never shown on screen. Minor, but worth noting.
