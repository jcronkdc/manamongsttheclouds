# Protagonist Name Options

Your world draws from Finnish, Slavic, and Old Norse roots. Every name in your story carries meaning: Jalo (noble), Soturi (warrior), Varas (evokes avarice/theft), Vero (truth), Baara (harsh, cutting). The protagonist's name should carry the same weight.

---

## Top 5 Recommendations

### 1. AELO (ah-EH-lo)
**Meaning in-world:** "Breath of remembering"
**Why it works:** Breath connects to clouds and air — he is the man amongst the clouds. "Remembering" connects directly to the magic system (magic IS memory in this version). It's soft and gentle — like the boy himself — but has a quiet, ancient gravity to it. It sounds like it belongs whispered in prophecy.

### 2. VAEL (VAYL)
**Meaning in-world:** "Of the giving spirit"
**Why it works:** It mirrors VARAS — same "Va-" prefix. Varas = "of the taking spirit." Vael = "of the giving spirit." The symmetry is thematically perfect: the taker and the giver, born from the same root, destined to destroy each other. Every time their names are spoken in the same sentence, the reader feels the opposition.

### 3. SOREN (SOH-ren)
**Meaning:** Old Norse — "one who endures"
**Why it works:** Nordic feel fits the world. It's a name that works for a small, beaten-down boy AND for a man who stands against a tyrant. There's iron in it. It doesn't sound heroic — it sounds stubborn. Survival. Endurance. That's his arc.

### 4. KAEL (KAYL)
**Meaning in-world:** "Vessel" or "The Hollow"
**Why it works:** He's been hollowed out by the herbs — emptied of magic his whole life. But a vessel is made to be filled. The name carries irony: everyone thinks "hollow" means empty, broken. But it means he has the capacity to hold more than anyone else alive. When his magic finally wakes, the name transforms from a description of weakness into a description of terrifying potential.

### 5. ERETH (EH-reth)
**Meaning in-world:** "Still water"
**Why it works:** Gentle, deep, reflects everything around it. Still water is the most deceptive thing in nature — it looks passive, but it holds entire worlds beneath the surface. It also connects to the Sea People's philosophy. A beautiful, quiet name for a boy who contains a storm.

## My Recommendation

**AELO** or **VAEL**. If you want the name to carry poetic weight on its own, go with Aelo — it sounds like wind through ancient halls. If you want the name to be a weapon in the narrative, a mirror held up to the villain every time they share a page, go with Vael.
