# THE WORLD — Regions, Geography & Culture

---

## The Shape of the World

The world is divided into seven regions, each defined by its primary elemental memory — the deepest, oldest memory embedded in the land itself. These regions are separated by massive walls of crystallized elemental magic called **The Rememberings** — natural formations that arose over millennia as the world's memory concentrated at geological boundaries. Each wall is a monument to its element: the wall surrounding the Sea region is a towering curtain of perpetually falling water that never reaches the ground. The wall of the Core is a ridge of stone so dense that sound cannot pass through it.

Before Varas, travel between regions was common. The walls had gates — natural passages maintained by joint magical effort. Under Varas, the gates were sealed. The Mrak Patrol guards each one. Travel between regions is now illegal without the King's seal.

The castle sits at the center of the world, where all seven regions converge — a place called **The Murkr** (Old Norse: darkness). It was not always dark. When the world was in balance, this convergence was the brightest place on earth — where all seven elemental memories harmonized. Under Varas, it has become a dead zone. The stolen magic has created a void at the center, and the land around the castle has withered into perpetual twilight.

## THE SEVEN REGIONS

### 1. The Canopy (Lehti)

**Element:** Wood / Life
**Magic:** The Know, The Greensong
**Landscape:** Ancient forest so vast that the floor has not seen sunlight in centuries. The trees are enormous — some with trunks wide enough to hold a village inside. Life here is vertical. People live in the mid-canopy, connected by rope bridges and carved stairways that spiral up the trunks. The forest floor is sacred and forbidden — it is where the dead are laid, and where the oldest memories in the region live.

**Culture:** The Pure Ones. A society built on observation, philosophy, and restraint. They believe magic should be listened to, not commanded. Their warriors follow a code similar to bushido — honor above survival, silence above speech, service above self. Children are raised communally; no child belongs to one family but to the forest itself. At age twelve, children descend to the forest floor for the first time in a ceremony called **The Listening** — they sit in the dark among the roots and the dead and try to hear the Know for the first time.

Those who hear become practitioners. Those who don't become stewards — equally honored, tasked with maintaining the physical structures of the Canopy.

Some warriors practice **The Greensong** — a form of trance-running where they synchronize their heartbeat with the forest's rhythm. A Greensinger can travel at extraordinary speed for days without rest, moving through the forest as if the trees themselves are carrying them. They feel no hunger, no fatigue, no pain. When they stop, the debt hits all at once. A Greensinger who runs for a week will sleep for a month.

**Under Varas:** The Canopy has been the hardest region to subjugate. The forest itself resists intrusion — paths shift, markers vanish, and Mrak Patrols sent into the deep Canopy tend not to return. Varas has resorted to burning the edges, but the old trees do not catch easily. The Pure Ones fight a quiet guerrilla war, never engaging directly, simply making the forest inhospitable to outsiders.

**Mentor character:** Mira, the silent warrior-monk. Teaches Aelo The Know.

### 2. The Core (Syvä)

**Element:** Earth / Stone
**Magic:** The Mold
**Landscape:** A vast, rocky continent of canyons, mesas, and caverns. The surface is harsh — wind-scoured stone, sparse vegetation, brutal sun. But the true Core is underground. The Molders have carved cities deep beneath the crust over thousands of years — cathedrals of stone, lit by bioluminescent fungi and veins of crystal that pulse with ambient magical memory. The deeper you go, the older the stone, and the stronger the Mold magic.

**Culture:** Practical, communal, loud. Core people value craft above all else — the ability to make something useful and beautiful from raw material. Their greatest artists are Molders who reshape stone into sculptures so detailed they seem alive. Their engineers build structures that can withstand earthquakes because they ask the stone to _remember being unbroken_.

Meals are eaten together, always. Solitude is considered a mild form of illness. Arguments are settled by building contests — whoever creates the more impressive structure wins the dispute. This sounds charming, but the structures are often enormous and the competitions can last weeks.

Core people feel weak on the surface. With earth only at their feet instead of all around them, their connection to the Mold is attenuated. They compensate with brute physical strength — generations of underground labor have made them the largest, densest people in the world.

**Under Varas:** The Core was conquered through its own tunnels. Varas collapsed the three main shafts connecting the surface to the deep cities, trapping thousands below. Those who surrendered were allowed to dig their way out. Those who didn't are still down there — entire communities living in sealed caverns, surviving on fungus and recycled water, waiting for a liberation that hasn't come in decades.

**Mentor character:** Torvus, the numb giant who can reshape mountains but can't feel his own skin. Teaches Aelo The Mold.

### 3. The Clouds (Pilvi)

**Element:** Air / Sky
**Magic:** The Sing (latent, ancestral), The Move, The Guide
**Landscape:** The Cloud People live on floating formations — masses of stone held aloft by ancient Move magic so old it has become self-sustaining. The stones were lifted into the sky by the first Cloud Movers centuries ago, and the air remembers them being there, so they stay. Some formations are small — a single family on a platform the size of a house. Others are massive, carrying entire towns. They drift slowly across the sky, following currents that the Cloud Guides read like roads.

The view from the Clouds is endless. On clear days, you can see three regions at once. The air is thin and cold. Light behaves differently here — sunrises last for hours, and the Blue Sun (which appears once a year) is visible from the Clouds for a full day longer than anywhere else.

**Culture:** The Cloud People are light, quick, and widely considered strange by everyone else. They speak softly — sound carries differently at altitude. They value vision (both literal and metaphorical) above all else. Their elders are called **Seers**, not because they predict the future but because they see things from a height that others cannot — patterns in the world that only become visible from above.

They carry vials of liquid — **Cloudmilk**, a substance distilled from condensation gathered at extreme altitude. It has mild restorative properties and is a controlled substance under Varas's regime. In combat, Cloud warriors fight like hummingbirds — darting, striking, disappearing. Their small frames and Move magic make them nearly impossible to hit. They inflict death through speed and precision, not force.

**Under Varas:** The hardest region to control because it's hard to conquer the sky. Varas has resorted to using Burn practitioners to create updrafts and storms that destabilize the floating stones. Several formations have been crashed, killing everyone aboard. The threat alone keeps most Cloud People compliant. A resistance exists — small, mobile, operating from formations that drift through unclaimed sky.

**Connection to the protagonist:** Aelo was born here. The nursery where Jalo took him was on a Cloud formation. His mother was a Cloud Singer — one of the last people to carry the Sing strongly in her blood. This is the region where the final confrontation begins, and where Fletcher's Great Elder Stone is hidden — suspended in the heart of the highest Cloud formation, singing a note that only Fletcher's blood can hear.

### 4. The Sea (Bahar)

**Element:** Water / Tide
**Magic:** The Guide, The Heal
**Landscape:** A vast coastline wrapped around a dark, cold ocean. The Sea People live on the shore and on great floating platforms anchored to the seabed — entire communities that rise and fall with the tide. The deep ocean is unexplored and feared. Things live in the depths that predate magic — creatures whose memory is so old it resists all communion. The Hollow Whales that surface occasionally are the largest living things in the world, and they do not respond to the Know.

**Culture:** Tidal. Everything about the Sea People follows rhythms — their schedules, their moods, their decisions. They measure time by the rise and fall of the sun and moon, whom they worship as the Twin Gods (they laugh at all other claims of divinity). They are slow to act and slow to anger, but when roused, they are relentless — like a tide that doesn't care about the castle in its path.

They are the world's best Healers. The Sea People believe that water is the world's first memory — that everything alive began in the ocean, and so water remembers every form of life that has ever existed. Their Healers work with water, applying it to wounds and asking it to remind the flesh of wholeness. Sea Healing is gentler and more effective than any other school, but it is also slower. A Sea Healer will not rush.

They also have **Guide magic** in their bones — an innate sense of direction and trajectory. Every Sea Person always knows which way the ocean is, even blindfolded, even underground. The rare Guides among them can sense the trajectory of the future — not prophecy, but momentum. The feeling of a wave about to break.

**Under Varas:** Conquered by controlling the narwhal harvest. Narwhal horn is a key material in Elder Stone construction (it resonates with memory in a way no other organic material does). Varas controls the narwhal hunting grounds off the coast of Bahar, and any Sea Person who wants to eat must participate in the harvest. The Sea People's slow nature made them poor revolutionaries — by the time they agreed that resistance was necessary, the Mrak Patrol had already fortified every port.

**Mentor character:** Laine, the young Guide who can see the future and wishes she couldn't. Descendant (unknowingly) of Essa, the mortal Sereth loved. Teaches Aelo The Guide.

### 5. The Volcano (Kuuma)

**Element:** Fire / Heat
**Magic:** The Burn
**Landscape:** A chain of active and dormant volcanoes, connected by rivers of slowly cooling lava. The land is black and red — obsidian plains, sulfur vents, basalt cliffs. It is beautiful in a terrifying way. The air shimmers with heat. Plants here are fire-adapted — thick-skinned, low to the ground, some growing directly in warm stone. The Volcano People's settlements are built into the calderas of dormant volcanoes, using the geothermal heat for everything from cooking to metalwork.

**Culture:** The Volcano People are the world's smiths and metalworkers. Their thick skin (a generational adaptation to centuries of Burn magic — their bodies remember heat as home) makes them uniquely suited to working with molten metal. Their blades, armor, and tools are the finest in the world.

They are direct to the point of bluntness. Diplomacy is not a Volcano trait. They value action over words, heat over light, and the present over the past or future. They are also deeply loyal — once a Volcano Person commits to a cause or a person, they will walk through literal fire for them. Betrayal is the only unforgivable crime.

Their weakness is cold. It stiffens their heat-adapted bodies, slows their movements, and makes their thickened skin brittle. A Volcano warrior in winter is a liability. This is why they rarely leave their region — and why Varas stations them exclusively in the Murkr, where the castle's heat keeps them operational.

**Under Varas:** Many Volcano People serve willingly. Varas offered them the one thing they couldn't make for themselves: purpose beyond the forge. He gave them titles, authority, and enemies to fight. The Mrak Patrol's shock troops are overwhelmingly Volcano People. This complicity is the Volcano region's great shame — and the reason other regions mistrust them even after liberation.

### 6. The Desert (Kuiva)

**Element:** Sand / Absence
**Magic:** The Burn (diminished), fragments of all other disciplines
**Landscape:** An endless expanse of sand, rock, and wind. Water exists only in deep wells and underground rivers. The Desert is what happens when a region's elemental memory is damaged — long ago, the Desert was green. A catastrophic magical event (possibly related to Fletcher's destruction of the scrolls) burned the memory of life out of the land. The sand remembers being something else, but it has forgotten what. This gives the Desert its defining magical property: it is a place where magic behaves unpredictably. Spells fizzle. The Know returns false readings. Mold magic produces wrong shapes. The world's memory here is **scrambled**.

**Culture:** Survivors. The Desert People are defined by scarcity. They waste nothing — not water, not food, not words, not magic. Their magic is a patchwork — weak fragments of every discipline, none strong enough to master. A Desert magician might have a whisper of the Know, a breath of the Move, a spark of the Burn. They combine these fragments into a discipline unique to their region called **The Weave** — the art of braiding weak magics together into something functional. It is the most technically demanding form of magic in the world, and practitioners from other regions cannot do it. It requires an intimacy with failure that only the Desert teaches.

They are nomadic, following underground water sources. Their caravans are mobile communities — everything they own is designed to be carried. Their greatest treasures are stories, told around fires at night, passed from generation to generation. They believe that the Desert will remember what it was when someone tells it the right story.

**Under Varas:** Mostly ignored. The Desert is too large, too hostile, and too magically unreliable for Varas to bother conquering. He considers it a wasteland and its people irrelevant. This is his mistake. The Desert People have been quietly harboring refugees, hidden magicians, and fugitive bloodlines for decades. The resistance's supply lines run through the Desert, and Varas has never thought to look there.

### 7. The Ming (Mieli)

**Element:** Mind / Thought
**Magic:** The Know (advanced), unique psychic disciplines
**Landscape:** Rolling hills and still lakes, perpetually shrouded in mist. The Ming region feels dreamlike — colors are muted, sounds are dampened, and time seems to move at a different pace. This is not metaphorical. The concentration of Know magic in the Ming is so dense that the land itself has become semi-conscious. The mist isn't weather — it's the region _thinking_.

**Culture:** The Ming Men (and Women) are scholars, philosophers, and psychics. They live in monasteries built around the still lakes, which they use as mirrors for deep meditation. Their version of the Know goes far beyond truth-sensing — the most advanced Ming practitioners can enter another person's memories and walk through them like rooms in a house. They can plant suggestions, erase trauma, and (in the worst cases) rewrite a person's identity entirely.

This makes them both the most trusted and most feared people in the world. A Ming Healer can cure madness. A Ming weaponizer can create it.

They prize emotional equilibrium above all else. Outbursts are considered a sign of underdeveloped practice. The ideal Ming practitioner feels everything and shows nothing — not suppression, but _integration_. They believe that all emotions are memories of past experiences, and that mastering the Know means understanding that your feelings are echoes, not commands.

**Under Varas:** The Ming were the first region Varas targeted, because they were the only people who could Know him — who could feel the void where his empathy should be and identify him as what he is. He sent The Knife with an army to the Ming monasteries before he even held his first council meeting. Many were killed. Those who survived went underground — literally, into caves beneath the still lakes where the mist is so thick that even Varas's draining stones cannot penetrate.

The Ming resistance is the most organized and most patient. They are waiting. They have Known this moment was coming for generations.

## THE MURKR — The Center

The convergence point. Where all seven regions meet. Once the brightest, most magically resonant place in the world — where the Elder Stones naturally formed, where the Knower Ceremonies were held in the old days, where King Fletcher's court stood.

Now: dead. The land around Varas's castle has been drained of all elemental memory. The grass is grey. The trees are petrified. The rivers are dry. The sky is a permanent dusk — not dark enough for stars, not light enough for shadows. It is a place where magic goes to die.

The castle itself is a grotesque monument to Varas's power. Originally a modest stone keep built by Fletcher's descendants, Varas has Molded it over centuries into a sprawling, organic nightmare — towers that curve like ribs, walls that pulse faintly with the stolen magic flowing through them, corridors that rearrange themselves according to Varas's will. The castle is, in a sense, alive — fed by the same Blood Vine network that feeds the King.

At its heart: The Chamber. Varas's collection. The rows of drained prisoners. The Elder Stones. And behind the locked door, the entombed King Aldric and Prince Vero.

## FAUNA

### Flash Rat

**Origin:** The Core (Syvä) — deep cavern systems below the crust
**Size:** Fox-to-small-dog. Roughly two feet long nose to tail, low-slung, built for speed in tight spaces. Deceptively heavy — Core bone density. A grown Flash Rat weighs as much as a large terrier despite its lean frame.

**Appearance:** Hairless. Skin the color of wet slate — grey-blue, thin enough to see the dark vascular map underneath. The skull is narrow, almost wedge-shaped, with oversized ears that are translucent and veined, constantly rotating like a bat's. The eyes are small, pale, and near-useless — Core Flash Rats evolved in total darkness and navigate almost entirely by scent and vibration. The whiskers are extraordinary: twelve to sixteen filaments, each as long as the animal's head, rigid as wire, fanning outward in a permanent splay. They do not twitch. They _listen_. Each whisker is a receiver — attuned to ambient magical resonance the way a tuning fork is attuned to a note.

**The Flash:** The name comes from the bioluminescent vein network visible through the skin. In the Core's deep caverns, Flash Rats produce a sudden, bright pulse of blue-white light along their entire body when they detect a magical signature — the same bioluminescent chemistry as the fungi and crystal veins that light the underground cities. In darkness, a Flash Rat acquiring a scent looks like a crack of lightning pressed into the shape of an animal. The flash is involuntary. The stronger the magical signature, the brighter and more frequent the pulse. Near a powerful source, the rat glows continuously — a sustained, flickering luminescence that makes the animal look like it's burning from inside.

**Behavior:** Singularly obsessive. A Flash Rat that has acquired a magical signature cannot be redirected. It will pursue the scent until the source is found or the animal dies of exhaustion. They do not eat during pursuit. They do not sleep. They scream — a thin, sustained, high-pitched keen that is less a vocalization than a vibration, produced by the throat and amplified by the oversized ears, audible from a quarter mile in open terrain. The scream intensifies with proximity to the target. Near a strong source, the screaming becomes continuous and the animal enters a state of frenzied overstimulation — rolling, frothing, clawing at the ground, driven beyond its neurological capacity by the beauty of what it can smell.

They are not aggressive. They have no interest in combat, food, or other animals. They want one thing: to be near the source. This makes them perfect trackers and terrible companions. Handlers use thick leather leashes and muzzles. The Mrak Patrol keeps them in kennel wagons — iron-barred cages on wheels — because an unleashed Flash Rat will run itself to death.

**Under Varas:** In the old world, Flash Rats were a curiosity — a bioluminescent pest in the Core's deep tunnels, attracted to Molders at work the way moths are attracted to light. The Mrak Patrol recognized their utility and began breeding them in captivity for pursuit operations. The breeding program stripped out the last traces of wildness and amplified the tracking compulsion. Captive-bred Flash Rats are more sensitive, more obsessive, and more fragile than their wild ancestors. They burn out faster. Most tracking rats survive three or four pursuits before the neurological damage accumulates and they stop responding to anything at all — alive but empty, the whiskers still and the light gone, staring at nothing.

## TRANSPORT & TECHNOLOGY

- **Saddle Birds:** Large avian beasts used for medium-distance travel, primarily by the Cloud People and wealthy merchants. Temperamental. Bond with a single rider.
- **Flying Ships:** Vessels held aloft by Elder Stones embedded in the hull and Move magic channeled through the mast. Rare and expensive. Varas controls most of them. The resistance has stolen a few.
- **Greensong Running:** The Canopy's method of rapid land travel. Not a technology — a discipline.
- **The Weave-Roads:** Desert trade routes where Weavers have braided small magics into the sand itself, creating paths that are always cool, always flat, and always point toward water. Invisible to anyone who doesn't know the Weave.
- **Wormholes:** Theoretical. The Ming believe that if you could fold the world's memory — convince two distant places that they remember being the same place — you could step between them. No one has achieved this. Sereth knows it's possible but has never shared the knowledge.
- **Enchanted Communication Devices:** Small Elder Stone fragments, paired, that can transmit short bursts of emotional memory over distance. Not words — feelings. A soldier can send a pulse of "danger" to a paired stone held by a commander miles away. Varas's court uses more refined versions that transmit crude images. The resistance has been trying to build their own.
