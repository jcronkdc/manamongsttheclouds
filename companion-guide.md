# MAN AMONGST THE CLOUDS — Reader Companion Guide

_A comprehensive guide to the world, its magic, and its people._

---

## I. THE MAGIC SYSTEM — Magic Is Memory

### The Core Principle

Magic is not energy. It is not force. It is **memory**.

The world remembers everything. Every stone remembers the mountain it was part of. Every river remembers the glacier that bore it. Every flame remembers the first fire that ever burned. Magic is the ability to commune with these memories — to listen to the world's remembering, and to speak back to it. To ask the stone to remember a different shape. To remind flesh of what it was before the wound. To ask the air to remember a different arrangement.

When the gods created the world, they poured their own memories into it. Magic, then, is the language the gods used to speak the world into being — and King Fletcher was the first mortal given the ability to hear it.

### The Seven Disciplines

Each discipline is a way of listening to a different kind of memory. Every discipline carries a cost — because magic always demands sacrifice.

#### 1. The Know

- **What it is:** Listening to the memory of living things — their truth, intent, emotion, and history.
- **What it looks like:** A Knower can feel whether someone is lying. A powerful Knower can read the shape of a person's past by touching them. The greatest Knowers can feel the emotional residue left in places — the sorrow soaked into a battlefield, the joy embedded in a wedding hall.
- **Cost:** Knowers absorb what they read. Every truth they pull from another person leaves a shadow in them. Overuse leads to emotional contamination — a Knower can lose track of which feelings are theirs.
- **Limit:** You cannot Know someone whose will is stronger than yours. You cannot Know the dead.

#### 2. The Mold

- **What it is:** Speaking to stone, earth, metal, and wood — asking it to remember a different shape.
- **What it looks like:** A Molder can reshape a wall into a doorway, coax iron into a blade without a forge, raise pillars from bedrock. The greatest Molders built the underground cities of the Core.
- **Cost:** Every act of Molding costs a physical memory — the feeling of your own body. Molders slowly lose sensation. Their fingers go numb first, then their hands, then their arms. Old Molders can't feel rain on their skin or heat from a fire.
- **Limit:** You cannot Mold living flesh. You cannot Mold what you cannot touch or see.

#### 3. The Heal

- **What it is:** Reminding flesh and bone of wholeness — what it was before the wound.
- **What it looks like:** A Healer places hands on the wounded and listens to the body's memory of health. Bones knit. Skin closes. Poison is remembered as foreign and expelled.
- **Cost:** Healers take the memory of the wound into themselves. Not the wound — the _memory_ of the pain. Over time, Healers accumulate a library of phantom suffering.
- **Limit:** You cannot Heal death. Once the body's memory has fully dispersed, the flesh forgets what it was and cannot be reminded. **You cannot Heal yourself** — you cannot be both the listener and the speaker. This is the cruelest rule of Healing.

#### 4. The Move

- **What it is:** Asking space and air to remember a different arrangement of things within it.
- **What it looks like:** Objects lifted, hurled, rearranged. A skilled Mover can redirect arrows in flight or hold a person immobile by asking the air around them to remember being solid.
- **Cost:** Movers lose spatial memory — their sense of where they are in the world. Overuse leads to episodes of disorientation. Old Movers get lost in their own homes.
- **Limit:** You cannot Move something you cannot perceive. Weight matters. You cannot Move magic itself.

#### 5. The Guide

- **What it is:** Listening to the memory of paths — where things have been and where they are going. The echo of trajectories.
- **What it looks like:** A Guide always knows which direction they face. A skilled Guide can track a person across a continent. The greatest Guides can sense the trajectory of the future — not prophecy, but the world's momentum.
- **Cost:** Guides lose their memory of home. They can find anything in the world except where they came from.
- **Limit:** The future is not fixed. Deliberate unpredictability clouds Guide-sight entirely.

#### 6. The Burn

- **What it is:** Speaking to fire and heat — awakening the world's memory of the first flame that burned at creation.
- **What it looks like:** Flame summoned from nothing. Temperatures raised or lowered (cold is the memory of fire's absence).
- **Cost:** Burners lose emotional warmth. They become cold inside — detached, analytical, unable to feel joy or love or grief with the same intensity. They can summon a wall of fire but cannot feel the warmth of an embrace. This is the loneliest magic.
- **Limit:** Fire is hungry and remembers being fed. It always wants more. Burners who lose concentration are consumed by their own flame.

#### 7. The Sing (The Rarest)

- **What it is:** Speaking to all memory at once. Harmony. Balance. The music the world makes when every memory resonates together. This is creation magic — the power the gods used to build reality.
- **What it looks like:** No one alive has seen it in centuries. Legend says King Fletcher could Sing — and that the Great Elder Stone is a crystallized Song, a single perfect note frozen in time.
- **Cost:** Everything. To Sing is to pour your entire self into the world's memory. You don't lose specific memories — you lose _yourself_. You become part of the world's remembering.
- **Limit:** Only those of Fletcher's bloodline can hear the Song.

### Rules That Cannot Be Broken

1. **You cannot Heal yourself.** You cannot be both the listener and the speaker.
2. **You cannot raise the dead.** Once memory disperses fully, it returns to the world.
3. **Magic always costs memory.** There are no exceptions. Even stolen magic costs the victim's memory.
4. **The Sing requires total sacrifice.** No one has ever Sung and remained themselves.
5. **The world resists being lied to.** Memory can be reshaped, not fabricated.
6. **Stolen magic degrades.** It will never be as strong as pure magic, and it always demands more.
7. **Blood cannot be hidden from blood.** Fletcher's descendants will always resonate with the Elder Stones.

## II. STOLEN MAGIC — The Drain

King Varas discovered that Elder Stones could be used in reverse. Instead of storing memory willingly, you could _extract_ it — ripping memory from a living person and pouring it into the stone, then consuming it yourself.

### How It Works

- The victim is connected to an Elder Stone through Blood Vine — a parasitic plant that latches onto the nervous system and acts as a conduit.
- The stone pulls memory from the victim: their magic, their experiences, their identity.
- The practitioner draws from the stone, using the stolen memory as fuel.

### Why It's Weaker

- Stolen memory is **corrupted**. A memory given freely retains its shape and power. A memory ripped away is damaged — like a page torn from a book. You get the words but not the meaning.
- Each transfer degrades the memory further. Copies of copies.
- Corrupted memory also corrupts the user physically, over time.

## III. MAGICAL OBJECTS & PHENOMENA

### Elder Stones

Ancient stones at the intersection of all seven disciplines — the world's oldest memories crystallized into physical form. Most were created naturally over millions of years. A few were created through the Sing.

**Properties:**

- They can store memory willingly given (the original purpose)
- They can extract memory forcibly (Varas's perversion)
- They amplify magical ability when in contact with a practitioner
- They resonate with Fletcher's bloodline — literally hum in their presence

### The Great Elder Stone

The one Fletcher poured himself into. The most powerful object in the world. Contains the complete memory of the Sing — the music of creation itself. Hidden among the clouds, singing a single perfect note that has not faded in three hundred years.

### Calibration Stones

Small, inert Elder Stones stripped of capacity, used by surveyors to measure ambient magical density in a region. They do not normally produce sound.

### Blood Vine

A parasitic plant that grows only in darkness and feeds on magical residue. In nature, it attaches to Elder Stones and slowly siphons ambient memory. Varas weaponized it as a conduit for forced extraction.

**Properties:**

- Latches onto the nervous system through the skin
- Creates a bridge between the victim's memory and the Elder Stone
- The victim feels the extraction as the loss of self — they forget things in real-time
- Prolonged exposure leaves the victim in a vegetative state — alive but empty

**In the Knower Ceremony:** Under the old ways, a thin strand was briefly applied to read a child's magical potential without harm. Under Varas, thick coils are used.

### The Blood-mark

An amber mark that sometimes appears on the inner wrist, visible only in certain light. It pulses with a rhythm like a heartbeat. The mark is not a scar or a tattoo — it lives in the skin, integral to the body's structure. Its origin and meaning are tied to the oldest orders of the realm.

### Cloudmilk

A substance distilled from condensation gathered at extreme altitude. Mild restorative properties. A controlled substance under the current regime.

### The Blue Sun

A celestial event that occurs once each year, during which the Knower Ceremonies are traditionally held. Visible from the Clouds for a full day longer than anywhere else.

### The Blood Moon

The astronomical event that precedes the Blue Sun. Once a time of celebration; now a night of dread.

## IV. THE WORLD — Regions, Geography & Culture

### The Shape of the World

The world is divided into seven regions, each defined by its primary elemental memory — the deepest, oldest memory embedded in the land itself. The regions are separated by massive walls of crystallized elemental magic called **The Rememberings**. Each wall is a monument to its element: the wall surrounding the Sea region is a towering curtain of perpetually falling water that never reaches the ground; the wall of the Core is a ridge of stone so dense that sound cannot pass through it.

Before Varas, travel between regions was common, with natural passages called gates maintained by joint magical effort. Under Varas, the gates were sealed and guarded by the Mrak Patrol.

---

### 1. The Canopy (Lehti)

**Element:** Wood / Life | **Magic:** The Know, The Greensong

An ancient forest so vast that the floor has not seen sunlight in centuries. Trees with trunks wide enough to hold a village inside. Life is vertical — people live in the mid-canopy, connected by rope bridges and carved stairways. The forest floor is sacred: where the dead are laid, and where the oldest memories live.

**The Pure Ones** — A society built on observation, philosophy, and restraint. Children are raised communally. At age twelve, children descend to the forest floor in a ceremony called **The Listening** — they sit in the dark among the roots and the dead and try to hear the Know for the first time. Those who hear become practitioners. Those who don't become stewards — equally honored.

**The Greensong** — A form of trance-running where warriors synchronize their heartbeat with the forest's rhythm. A Greensinger can travel at extraordinary speed for days. The forest itself carries them — branches extend, roots surface to form steps. When they stop, the debt hits all at once: a runner who goes a week will sleep for a month.

**Under Varas:** The hardest region to subjugate. The forest itself resists intrusion — paths shift, markers vanish. The Pure Ones fight a quiet guerrilla war, never engaging directly.

---

### 2. The Core (Syvä)

**Element:** Earth / Stone | **Magic:** The Mold

A vast, rocky continent of canyons, mesas, and caverns on the surface. The true Core is underground — cities carved deep beneath the crust over thousands of years, cathedrals of stone lit by bioluminescent fungi and crystal veins that pulse with ambient magical memory.

**Culture:** Practical, communal, loud. They value craft above all else. Meals are always eaten together; solitude is considered a mild form of illness. Arguments are settled by building contests. Core people are the largest, densest people in the world, built by generations of underground labor.

**Under Varas:** Conquered through its own tunnels. Varas collapsed the three main shafts, trapping thousands below. Entire communities survive in sealed caverns on fungus and recycled water.

---

### 3. The Clouds (Pilvi)

**Element:** Air / Sky | **Magic:** The Sing (ancestral), The Move, The Guide

The Cloud People live on floating formations — masses of stone held aloft by ancient Move magic so old the air remembers them being there. Some are small family platforms; others carry entire towns. They drift across the sky following currents that Cloud Guides read like roads. The view is endless. Sunrises last for hours.

**Culture:** Light, quick, widely considered strange. They speak softly — sound carries differently at altitude. Their elders are called **Seers**. In combat, Cloud warriors fight like hummingbirds — darting, striking, disappearing.

**Under Varas:** Varas uses Burn practitioners to create storms that destabilize the floating stones. Several have been crashed. A resistance operates from formations that drift through unclaimed sky.

---

### 4. The Sea (Bahar)

**Element:** Water / Tide | **Magic:** The Guide, The Heal

A vast coastline around a dark, cold ocean. The Sea People live on shore and on floating platforms anchored to the seabed that rise and fall with the tide. Everything follows rhythms — schedules, moods, decisions. They measure time by the sun and moon, whom they worship as the Twin Gods.

They are the world's best Healers. Their Healers work with water, asking it to remind the flesh of wholeness. Sea Healing is gentler and more effective than any other school, but also slower. Every Sea Person always knows which way the ocean is, even blindfolded.

The deep ocean is unexplored and feared. The **Hollow Whales** that surface occasionally are the largest living things in the world, and they do not respond to the Know.

**Under Varas:** Conquered by controlling the narwhal harvest. Narwhal horn resonates with memory in a way no other organic material does, making it key to Elder Stone construction.

---

### 5. The Volcano (Kuuma)

**Element:** Fire / Heat | **Magic:** The Burn

A chain of active and dormant volcanoes connected by rivers of slowly cooling lava. Obsidian plains, sulfur vents, basalt cliffs. The Volcano People are the world's smiths — their heat-adapted skin (a generational adaptation) makes them uniquely suited to working with molten metal.

**Culture:** Direct to the point of bluntness. Once committed to a cause, they walk through literal fire. Their weakness is cold — it stiffens their bodies and makes their thickened skin brittle.

**Under Varas:** Many serve willingly. Varas offered them purpose beyond the forge. The Mrak Patrol's shock troops are overwhelmingly Volcano People — a complicity that is the region's great shame.

---

### 6. The Desert (Kuiva)

**Element:** Sand / Absence | **Magic:** The Weave

An endless expanse of sand where the elemental memory has been damaged. Magic behaves unpredictably — spells fizzle, the Know returns false readings, Mold magic produces wrong shapes. The world's memory here is **scrambled**.

**Culture:** Survivors defined by scarcity. They waste nothing. Their magic is a patchwork — weak fragments of every discipline, none strong enough to master. They braid these fragments together in a unique discipline called **The Weave**. They are nomadic, following underground water. Their greatest treasures are stories, told around fires.

**The Weave-Roads** — Trade routes where Weavers have braided small magics into the sand, creating paths that are always cool, always flat, and always point toward water. Invisible to anyone who doesn't know the Weave.

**Under Varas:** Mostly ignored. The Desert is too large and too magically unreliable to bother conquering.

---

### 7. The Ming (Mieli)

**Element:** Mind / Thought | **Magic:** The Know (advanced), psychic disciplines

Rolling hills and still lakes, perpetually shrouded in mist. The concentration of Know magic is so dense that the land itself has become semi-conscious. The mist is the region _thinking_.

**Culture:** The Ming are scholars, philosophers, and psychics. Their advanced Know allows them to enter another person's memories and walk through them like rooms. They prize emotional equilibrium above all. The ideal practitioner feels everything and shows nothing — not suppression, but _integration_.

**Under Varas:** The first region targeted. Many were killed. Survivors went underground, into caves beneath the still lakes where the mist is too thick for draining stones to penetrate.

---

### The Murkr (Old Norse: "darkness")

The convergence point where all seven regions meet. Once the brightest, most magically resonant place on earth — where Elder Stones naturally formed and the Knower Ceremonies were held in the old days. Under Varas, it has become a dead zone. The grass is grey, the trees petrified, the rivers dry, the sky a permanent dusk.

The castle itself was originally a modest stone keep. Varas has Molded it over centuries into an organic nightmare — towers that curve like ribs, walls that pulse with stolen magic, corridors that rearrange according to his will.

## V. CREATURES & FAUNA

### Flash Rat

**Origin:** The Core — deep cavern systems

Fox-to-small-dog-sized, hairless, skin the color of wet slate. The skull is narrow with oversized translucent ears and extraordinary whiskers — twelve to sixteen rigid filaments that act as receivers attuned to ambient magical resonance. When they detect a magical signature, their entire body produces a bright pulse of blue-white bioluminescence — the "flash." Near a powerful source, the rat glows continuously.

A Flash Rat that has acquired a signature cannot be redirected. It will pursue until the source is found or the animal dies. They scream — a thin, sustained keen audible from a quarter mile. They are not aggressive; they simply want to be near the source.

Bred by the Mrak Patrol as trackers. Captive-bred rats are more sensitive and more fragile than wild ancestors. Most survive three or four pursuits before burning out.

### Saddle Bird

Large avian beasts used for medium-distance travel, primarily by the Cloud People and wealthy merchants. Temperamental. Bond with a single rider. A blue feather from a Saddle Bird is among the most beautiful things in the world.

### Hollow Whale

The largest living things in the world. They surface occasionally in the deep ocean off Bahar. Their memory is so ancient it resists all communion — the Know cannot read them, the Sing cannot reach them. The Sea People consider them sacred and unknowable. Their bones wash ashore and are used as building material for the floating platforms.

### Armored Ants

Ancient insects that march in slow columns through the loam. Patient, enduring, older than human memory. They are among the first creatures mentioned in the world's song.

### Hopper

A small, common brown bird of the Canopy with a bright black eye.

## VI. ORGANIZATIONS & FORCES

### The Vael Guard

An ancient order of warrior-healers sworn to protect King Fletcher's bloodline across generations. The Guard predates every kingdom in the current age. Members were chosen not for combat ability but for their willingness to sacrifice — every member was both a fighter and a Healer, absorbing the phantom pain of every wound they mended while being unable to heal their own.

**The Oath:** _"I am the wall between the fire and the song. I will burn before the song goes silent."_

The Guard was hunted to near-extinction during Varas's purge. Their blood-mark — an amber symbol on the inner wrist — connects Guardian to charge across any distance.

### The Mrak Patrol

King Varas's military force. Dark-armored soldiers stamped with the double-stone sigil of the King's authority. They guard the sealed gates between regions and carry out the King's orders, including the Knower Ceremonies. Their standard weapons include diamond-tipped spears (for Volcano infantry) and long, curved blades. They breed Flash Rats for pursuit operations and travel in kennel wagons — iron-barred cages on wheels.

### The Pure Ones

Warrior-monks of the Canopy. A society built on observation, philosophy, and restraint. They follow a code similar to bushido — honor above survival, silence above speech, service above self.

### Cloud Resistance

A small, mobile resistance force operating from Cloud formations that drift through unclaimed sky. Young, slight, wind-burned fighters adapted for altitude and speed.

## VIII. TRANSPORT & TECHNOLOGY

- **Saddle Birds** — Bonded avian mounts. Cloud People's primary transport.
- **Flying Ships** — Vessels held aloft by Elder Stones in the hull and Move magic through the mast. Rare and expensive. Varas controls most of them.
- **Greensong Running** — The Canopy's method of rapid land travel. Not a technology — a discipline.
- **The Weave-Roads** — Invisible Desert trade routes braided with small magics. Always cool, always flat, always pointing toward water.
- **Enchanted Communication Devices** — Paired Elder Stone fragments that transmit short bursts of emotional memory over distance. Not words — feelings. A soldier can send a pulse of "danger" to a paired stone miles away.

## X. THE PROPHECY

Carved into the base of the Great Elder Stone in the language of memory — felt rather than spoken:

> _One will come who carries the Song in his blood. He will hear the stone. He will find the note. And in Singing, he will remember what the world forgot._

The prophecy was carried by the Vael Guard — memorized, passed from captain to captain, generation to generation, a chain of whispered words stretching across three centuries.

## XI. KEY PHRASES & CEREMONIES

**The Vael Guard Oath:** _"I am the wall between the fire and the song. I will burn before the song goes silent. I will forget my own name before I forget theirs. I will carry what cannot be carried and hold what cannot be held and stand where no one can stand."_

**The Listening** — The Canopy ceremony in which twelve-year-old children descend to the sacred forest floor and sit in the dark among the roots and the dead, trying to hear the Know for the first time. Those who hear become practitioners. Those who don't become stewards — equally honored, tasked with maintaining the Canopy's structures.

**The Knower Ceremony (The Knowing)** — Once a year, under the Blue Sun, children are tested for magical ability by a Knower using an Elder Stone and Blood Vine. In the old days, it was a celebration with laughter and flowers. Under Varas, it has become something else entirely.
