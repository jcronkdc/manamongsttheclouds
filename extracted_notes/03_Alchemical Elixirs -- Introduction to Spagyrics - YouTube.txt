maybe you know this, however, it wasn't apparent in the video....but, as an old hand at making moonshine, the reason for distilling multiple times is to obtain pure alcohol. there are a number of chemicals produced by yeast in addition to alcohol. the second most abundant chemical is formaldehyde. when distilling, if you pay close attention to temp at the top of the distillation tower and flow/volume exiting the coil, you will find that the lighter components such as the formaldehyde will distill off before the alcohol at a slightly lower temp. the temp will remain constant  while the formaldehyde distills off and once the flow stops, the temp will go up to the temp at which alcohol evaporates and it will stabilize once again, this time the flow will be alcohol. The idea is to gather and throw out the formaldehyde as soon as the lower temp distillation flow stops. now, the  different evaporation temps are not perfect, they over lap as a percentage, and the boiling temp is affected by the concentration of solvents in the mixture..... so, you get about 80% of the formaldehyde to come off on the first lower temp distillation(a guestimate for the average water cooled coil still). in order to further purify the alcohol you must distil it several times....each time you will find there is less and less formaldehyde to throw out, and the temp will stay at the lower temp for shorter and shorter periods..... I found 5 distillations will produce no more than a few drops of formaldehyde.... be sure to allow some alcohol to start flowing before you remove the formaldehyde collection jug in order to flush the coil. note* do not use plastic coils as formaldehyde will dissolve the plastic, later contaminating the alcohol when it passes through the coil.﻿
