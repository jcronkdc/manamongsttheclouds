# Reader's Glossary

_A guide to the world, its magic, its people, and its creatures. No spoilers beyond Part One._

---

## The Magic System — Magic Is Memory

Magic is not energy. It is not force. It is **memory**.

The world remembers everything. Every stone remembers the mountain it was part of. Every river remembers the glacier that bore it. Every flame remembers the first fire that ever burned. Magic is the ability to commune with these memories — to listen to the world's remembering, and to speak back to it.

When the gods created the world, they poured their own memories into it. Magic, then, is the language the gods used to speak the world into being — and King Fletcher was the first mortal given the ability to hear it.

### The Seven Disciplines

Each discipline is a way of listening to a different kind of memory. Every discipline carries a cost — because magic always demands sacrifice.

**1. The Know** — Listening to the memory of living things — their truth, intent, emotion, and history. A Knower can feel whether someone is lying. The greatest Knowers can feel the emotional residue left in places — the sorrow soaked into a battlefield, the joy embedded in a wedding hall. _Cost:_ Knowers absorb what they read. Overuse leads to emotional contamination — a Knower can lose track of which feelings are theirs.

**2. The Mold** — Speaking to stone, earth, metal, and wood — asking it to remember a different shape. A Molder can reshape a wall into a doorway, coax iron into a blade without a forge, raise pillars from bedrock. _Cost:_ Every act of Molding costs a physical memory — the feeling of your own body. Molders slowly lose sensation. Old Molders can't feel rain on their skin or heat from a fire.

**3. The Heal** — Reminding flesh and bone of wholeness — what it was before the wound. A Healer places hands on the wounded and listens to the body's memory of health. Bones knit. Skin closes. _Cost:_ Healers take the memory of the wound into themselves. Not the wound — the _memory_ of the pain. Over time, Healers accumulate a library of phantom suffering. _Critical rule:_ You cannot Heal yourself. You cannot be both the listener and the speaker.

**4. The Move** — Asking space and air to remember a different arrangement of things within it. Objects lifted, hurled, rearranged. A skilled Mover can redirect arrows in flight or hold a person immobile by asking the air around them to remember being solid. _Cost:_ Movers lose spatial memory — their sense of where they are in the world. Old Movers get lost in their own homes.

**5. The Guide** — Listening to the memory of paths — where things have been and where they are going. A Guide always knows which direction they face. The greatest Guides can sense the trajectory of the future — not prophecy, but the world's momentum. _Cost:_ Guides lose their memory of home. They can find anything in the world except where they came from.

**6. The Burn** — Speaking to fire and heat — awakening the world's memory of the first flame that burned at creation. Flame summoned from nothing. Temperatures raised or lowered. _Cost:_ Burners lose emotional warmth. They become cold inside — detached, analytical, unable to feel joy or love or grief with the same intensity. This is the loneliest magic.

**7. The Sing** — Speaking to all memory at once. Harmony. Balance. The music the world makes when every memory resonates together. This is creation magic — the power the gods used to build reality. No one alive has seen it in centuries. Legend says King Fletcher could Sing — and that the Great Elder Stone is a crystallized Song, a single perfect note frozen in time. _Cost:_ Everything. To Sing is to pour your entire self into the world's memory.

### Rules That Cannot Be Broken

1. You cannot Heal yourself.
2. You cannot raise the dead.
3. Magic always costs memory. There are no exceptions.
4. The Sing requires total sacrifice.
5. The world resists being lied to. Memory can be reshaped, not fabricated.
6. Stolen magic degrades.
7. Blood cannot be hidden from blood.

## Stolen Magic — The Drain

King Varas discovered that Elder Stones could be used in reverse — ripping memory from a living person and pouring it into the stone, then consuming it yourself.

The victim is connected to an Elder Stone through Blood Vine — a parasitic plant that latches onto the nervous system and acts as a conduit. The stone pulls memory from the victim: their magic, their experiences, their identity. Stolen memory is corrupted. A memory given freely retains its shape and power. A memory ripped away is damaged — like a page torn from a book. Each transfer degrades the memory further. Copies of copies.

## Magical Objects & Phenomena

**Elder Stones** — Ancient stones at the intersection of all seven disciplines — the world's oldest memories crystallized into physical form. They can store memory willingly given, extract memory forcibly, and amplify magical ability. They resonate with Fletcher's bloodline.

**The Great Elder Stone** — The one Fletcher poured himself into. The most powerful object in the world. Contains the complete memory of the Sing. Hidden. Lost.

**Blood Vine** — A parasitic plant that grows only in darkness and feeds on magical residue. Varas weaponized it as a conduit for forced extraction. It latches onto the nervous system through the skin. Prolonged exposure leaves the victim in a vegetative state — alive but empty.

**The Blood-mark** — An amber mark that sometimes appears on the inner wrist, visible only in certain light. It pulses with a rhythm like a heartbeat. The mark is not a scar or a tattoo — it lives in the skin, integral to the body's structure.

**Cloudmilk** — A substance distilled from condensation gathered at extreme altitude. Mild restorative properties. A controlled substance under the current regime.

**The Blue Sun** — A celestial event that occurs once each year, during which the Knower Ceremonies are traditionally held.

**The Blood Moon** — The astronomical event that precedes the Blue Sun. Once a time of celebration; now a night of dread.

**Calibration Stones** — Small, inert Elder Stones stripped of capacity, used by surveyors to measure ambient magical density. They do not normally produce sound.

## The Seven Regions

The world is divided into seven regions, each defined by its primary elemental memory. The regions are separated by massive walls of crystallized elemental magic called **The Rememberings**. Each wall is a monument to its element. Before Varas, travel between regions was common. Under Varas, the gates were sealed and guarded by the Mrak Patrol.

### 1. The Canopy (Lehti)

**Element:** Wood / Life | **Magic:** The Know, The Rootrun

An ancient forest so vast that the floor has not seen sunlight in centuries. Trees with trunks wide enough to hold a village inside. Life is vertical — people live in the mid-canopy, connected by rope bridges and carved stairways. The forest floor is sacred: where the dead are laid, and where the oldest memories live.

**The Pure Ones** — A society built on observation, philosophy, and restraint. Children are raised communally. At age twelve, children descend to the forest floor in a ceremony called **The Listening** — they sit in the dark among the roots and the dead and try to hear the Know for the first time.

**The Rootrun** — A form of trance-running where warriors synchronize their heartbeat with the forest's rhythm. A Rootrunner can travel at extraordinary speed for days. When they stop, the debt hits all at once: a runner who goes a week will sleep for a month.

Under Varas, the Canopy has been the hardest region to subjugate. The forest itself resists intrusion — paths shift, markers vanish.

### 2. The Core (Syvä)

**Element:** Earth / Stone | **Magic:** The Mold

A vast, rocky continent of canyons, mesas, and caverns on the surface. The true Core is underground — cities carved deep beneath the crust over thousands of years, cathedrals of stone lit by bioluminescent fungi and crystal veins that pulse with ambient magical memory.

Practical, communal, loud. They value craft above all else. Meals are always eaten together; solitude is considered a mild form of illness. Arguments are settled by building contests. Core people are the largest, densest people in the world, built by generations of underground labor.

Under Varas, the Core was conquered through its own tunnels. Entire communities survive in sealed caverns on fungus and recycled water.

### 3. The Clouds (Pilvi)

**Element:** Air / Sky | **Magic:** The Move, The Guide

The Cloud People live on floating formations — masses of stone held aloft by ancient Move magic so old the air remembers them being there. Some are small family platforms; others carry entire towns. They drift across the sky following currents that Cloud Guides read like roads. The view is endless. Sunrises last for hours.

Light, quick, widely considered strange. They speak softly — sound carries differently at altitude. Their elders are called **Seers**. In combat, Cloud warriors fight like hummingbirds — darting, striking, disappearing.

Under Varas, several formations have been crashed using Burn storms. A resistance operates from formations that drift through unclaimed sky.

### 4. The Sea (Bahar)

**Element:** Water / Tide | **Magic:** The Guide, The Heal

A vast coastline around a dark, cold ocean. The Sea People live on shore and on floating platforms anchored to the seabed that rise and fall with the tide. Everything follows rhythms. They measure time by the sun and moon, whom they worship as the Twin Gods.

They are the world's best Healers. Their Healers work with water, asking it to remind the flesh of wholeness. Every Sea Person always knows which way the ocean is, even blindfolded.

The deep ocean is unexplored and feared. The **Hollow Whales** that surface occasionally are the largest living things in the world, and they do not respond to the Know.

Under Varas, the Sea was conquered by controlling the narwhal harvest. Narwhal horn resonates with memory, making it key to Elder Stone construction.

### 5. The Volcano (Kuuma)

**Element:** Fire / Heat | **Magic:** The Burn

A chain of active and dormant volcanoes connected by rivers of slowly cooling lava. Obsidian plains, sulfur vents, basalt cliffs. The Volcano People are the world's smiths — their heat-adapted skin makes them uniquely suited to working with molten metal.

Direct to the point of bluntness. Once committed to a cause, they walk through literal fire. Their weakness is cold — it stiffens their bodies and makes their thickened skin brittle.

Under Varas, many Volcano People serve willingly. The Mrak Patrol's shock troops are overwhelmingly Volcano People.

### 6. The Desert (Kuiva)

**Element:** Sand / Absence | **Magic:** The Weave

An endless expanse of sand where the elemental memory has been damaged. Magic behaves unpredictably. The world's memory here is scrambled.

Survivors defined by scarcity. They waste nothing. Their magic is a patchwork of weak fragments from every discipline, braided together in a unique discipline called **The Weave**. They are nomadic, following underground water. Their greatest treasures are stories, told around fires.

**The Weave-Roads** — Trade routes where Weavers have braided small magics into the sand, creating paths that are always cool, always flat, and always point toward water.

Under Varas, the Desert is mostly ignored.

### 7. The Ming (Mieli)

**Element:** Mind / Thought | **Magic:** The Know (advanced), psychic disciplines

Rolling hills and still lakes, perpetually shrouded in mist. The concentration of Know magic is so dense that the land itself has become semi-conscious. The mist is the region _thinking_.

The Ming are scholars, philosophers, and psychics. Their advanced Know allows them to enter another person's memories and walk through them like rooms. They prize emotional equilibrium above all.

Under Varas, the Ming were the first region targeted. Survivors went underground, into caves beneath the still lakes.

### The Murkr (Old Norse: "darkness")

The convergence point where all seven regions meet. Once the brightest, most magically resonant place on earth. Under Varas, it has become a dead zone. The grass is grey, the trees petrified, the rivers dry, the sky a permanent dusk.

## Creatures & Fauna

### Flash Rat

**Origin:** The Core — deep cavern systems

Fox-to-small-dog-sized, hairless, skin the color of wet slate. Oversized translucent ears and extraordinary whiskers — twelve to sixteen rigid filaments that act as receivers attuned to ambient magical resonance. When they detect a magical signature, their entire body produces a bright pulse of blue-white bioluminescence — the "flash." Near a powerful source, the rat glows continuously.

A Flash Rat that has acquired a signature cannot be redirected. It will pursue until the source is found or the animal dies. They scream — a thin, sustained keen audible from a quarter mile. They are not aggressive; they simply want to be near the source. The Mrak Patrol breeds them as trackers. Most captive-bred rats survive three or four pursuits before burning out — alive but empty, the whiskers still and the light gone.

### Saddle Bird

Large avian beasts used for medium-distance travel, primarily by the Cloud People and wealthy merchants. Temperamental. Bond with a single rider. A blue feather from a Saddle Bird is among the most beautiful things in the world.

### Hollow Whale

The largest living things in the world. They surface occasionally in the deep ocean off Bahar. Their memory is so ancient it resists all communion — the Know cannot read them, the Sing cannot reach them. The Sea People consider them sacred and unknowable. Their bones wash ashore and are used as building material.

### Armored Ants

Ancient insects that march in slow columns through the loam. Patient, enduring, older than human memory. Among the first creatures mentioned in the world's song.

### Hopper

A small, common brown bird of the Canopy with a bright black eye.

## Organizations & Forces

**The Vael Guard** — An ancient order of warrior-healers sworn to protect King Fletcher's bloodline across generations. Members were both fighters and Healers. Their oath: _"I am the wall between the fire and the song. I will burn before the song goes silent."_ The Guard was hunted to near-extinction during Varas's purge. Their blood-mark — an amber symbol on the inner wrist — connects Guardian to charge across any distance.

**The Mrak Patrol** — King Varas's military force. Dark-armored soldiers stamped with the double-stone sigil. They guard the sealed gates between regions, carry out the Knower Ceremonies, breed Flash Rats for pursuit, and travel with kennel wagons.

**The Pure Ones** — Warrior-monks of the Canopy. Honor above survival, silence above speech, service above self.

## Transport & Technology

- **Saddle Birds** — Bonded avian mounts. Cloud People's primary transport.
- **Flying Ships** — Vessels held aloft by Elder Stones and Move magic. Rare and expensive. Varas controls most of them.
- **Rootrunning** — The Canopy's method of rapid land travel. A discipline, not a technology.
- **The Weave-Roads** — Invisible Desert trade routes. Always cool, always flat, always pointing toward water.
- **Enchanted Communication Devices** — Paired Elder Stone fragments that transmit emotional memory over distance. Not words — feelings.

## Key Phrases & Ceremonies

**The Vael Guard Oath:** _"I am the wall between the fire and the song. I will burn before the song goes silent. I will forget my own name before I forget theirs. I will carry what cannot be carried and hold what cannot be held and stand where no one can stand."_

**The Listening** — The Canopy ceremony in which twelve-year-old children descend to the sacred forest floor and sit in the dark among the roots and the dead, trying to hear the Know for the first time.

**The Knower Ceremony (The Knowing)** — Once a year, under the Blue Sun, children are tested for magical ability by a Knower using an Elder Stone and Blood Vine. In the old days, it was a celebration with laughter and flowers. Under Varas, it has become something else entirely.
