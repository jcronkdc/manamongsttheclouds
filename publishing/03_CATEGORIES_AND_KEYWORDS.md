# Amazon KDP Categories & Keywords

## BISAC Categories (choose 3 on KDP)

### Primary
1. **FICTION / Fantasy / Epic**
   - This is your main shelf. Where Rothfuss, Sanderson, Hobb, and Kay live.

2. **FICTION / Fantasy / General**
   - Broader net. Captures literary fantasy readers who browse beyond "epic."

3. **FICTION / Literary**
   - Signals that this is not standard genre fare. Attracts the Le Guin / Kay / Clarke readers.

### Alternate options (if the above aren't available as exact matches on KDP):
- FICTION / Fantasy > Contemporary
- FICTION / Coming of Age
- FICTION / Magical Realism (stretch, but literary fantasy readers browse here)

## Amazon Keywords (7 keyword phrases — max 50 characters each)

These are the search terms readers type into Amazon. Each should be a phrase, not a single word.

1. `literary fantasy novel magic system`
2. `epic fantasy debut grief and memory`
3. `fantasy novel about sacrifice`
4. `found family fantasy guardian protector`
5. `villain with depth sympathetic antagonist`
6. `books like Name of the Wind Rothfuss`
7. `fantasy novel music magic singing`

### Why these work:
- **Keywords 1-3:** Describe the book's actual content for organic search
- **Keyword 4:** "Found family" is a massively popular search term in fantasy (Jalo/Aelo relationship)
- **Keyword 5:** Readers actively search for complex villains — Varas is a huge selling point
- **Keyword 6:** Comp title keyword — readers searching for "books like X" are your exact audience
- **Keyword 7:** Captures the music/singing magic niche (Rothfuss, V.E. Schwab, etc.)

## Comp Titles (for marketing copy, not KDP fields)

Use in your book description, social media, and pitch materials:

- **Patrick Rothfuss** (*The Name of the Wind*) — prose density, music-as-magic
- **Robin Hobb** (*Assassin's Apprentice*) — guardian/charge bond, emotional devastation
- **Guy Gavriel Kay** (*Tigana*) — memory as weapon, literary fantasy
- **Ursula K. Le Guin** (*A Wizard of Earthsea*) — moral weight of magic, restraint
- **Madeline Miller** (*The Song of Achilles*) — mythic register, love embedded in epic

### Pitch Formula:
> "For readers of [Rothfuss] and [Hobb] — a literary fantasy about memory, sacrifice, and a boy who can hear the world sing."

## Amazon A+ Content Suggestions (if enrolled in KDP)

Amazon allows enhanced product pages with images and formatted text. Recommended modules:

1. **"From the Author"** — personal note about why you wrote the book
2. **Comparison chart** — "If you liked X, you'll love Man Amongst the Clouds"
3. **Character profiles** — Aelo, Jalo, The Knife, Varas (with one-line hooks)
4. **World map** (if you commission one)
5. **Pull quote** — "I didn't want to be a monster. I wanted to hear the music." — King Varas
