## 2. Publishing & Distribution

### Primary Distribution (Day 1)

| Platform | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Amazon KDP** | Ebook + Paperback | Primary sales channel (~70% of all book sales) |
| **IngramSpark** | Paperback + Hardcover | Bookstore, library, and international distribution |
| **Draft2Digital** | Ebook | Wide distribution: Apple Books, Kobo, Barnes & Noble, Google Play |

### Strategy: KDP Select vs. Wide

**Recommended: Go wide from Day 1.**

Rationale:
- You already have a website ecosystem driving traffic — you don't want to lock readers into Amazon-only
- Your blockchain proof and Stillfire Press brand are about independence and ownership — KDP Select exclusivity contradicts that brand identity
- Wide distribution through IngramSpark is how you get into physical bookstores and libraries
- You can always enroll in KDP Select later for a 90-day promotional burst if needed

### Format Priority

1. **Ebook** (highest margin, fastest to market)
2. **Paperback** via KDP + IngramSpark (readers expect it)
3. **Hardcover** via IngramSpark (for collectors, libraries, gifts)
4. **Audiobook** (Phase 2 — Month 6+, see Long-Term Strategy)

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### Ebook
| Price | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **$4.99** at launch | Low enough to reduce friction for a debut author; high enough for 70% royalty tier on Amazon ($3.49 per sale). Signals value without intimidating new readers. |
| **$0.99** for promotions | Use for limited 48-hour spikes with email blasts and paid newsletter promos. |
| **$6.99** after 6 months | If reviews and traction support it, raise to standard fantasy pricing. |

### Paperback
| Price | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **$16.99** | Standard for 350+ page fantasy paperback. Printing cost ~$5–6 via KDP, giving ~$4–5 royalty per sale. Competitive with traditionally published fantasy. |

### Hardcover
| Price | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **$27.99** | Premium format. Printing cost ~$10–12 via IngramSpark. Positioned for collectors, gifts, and library acquisitions. |

### Companion Story (*Ash to Fury*)
| Price | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **Free** | Reader magnet. Offered free on the website and via email signup. Drives email list growth. Available on all retailers at $0.00 (permafree). |

## 4. Pre-Launch Phase (Months 1–3)

### Month 1: Foundation

**Cover & Design**
- Commission professional cover art. Budget $500–$2,000 for a quality fantasy cover. The cover sells the book — do not skimp here.
- Options: 99designs (competition format), Reedsy marketplace, or direct commission from a fantasy cover artist (check DeviantArt, ArtStation).
- Get the cover design brief (already written) to the artist immediately.
- Need: front cover, full wrap (front/back/spine for print), ebook cover.

**ISBNs**
- Purchase a block of 10 ISBNs from Bowker (~$295). One each for: ebook, paperback, hardcover, audiobook (future), large print (future). Having your own ISBNs means Stillfire Press is listed as publisher, not Amazon.
- Note: KDP provides free ISBNs, but those list the publisher as "Independently published." For brand credibility, use your own.

**Email List**
- Set up email marketing. Recommended: **MailerLite** (free up to 1,000 subscribers) or **Kit** (fka ConvertKit).
- Create a landing page on manamongsttheclouds.com for email signups.
- Reader magnet: Offer *Ash to Fury* as a free download in exchange for email signup.
- Goal: 500 subscribers before launch.

**Profiles**
- Create Amazon Author Central page.
- Create Goodreads author profile. Add the book as "upcoming."
- Claim BookBub author profile.

### Month 2: ARC Team & Content

**ARC (Advance Reader Copy) Team**
- Recruit 30–50 ARC readers. Sources:
  - Email list subscribers (ask who wants early access)
  - Facebook groups: search "fantasy ARC readers," "epic fantasy book club"
  - Reddit: r/Fantasy (follow their self-promo rules carefully)
  - BookSirens, BookSprout, or NetGalley (paid ARC distribution platforms)
- Send ARC copies (ebook format) 6–8 weeks before launch.
- Ask for honest reviews on Amazon and Goodreads on launch day.

**Content Creation**
- Write 3–5 blog posts / articles for the website:
  - "The Magic System of MATC" (world-building deep dive)
  - "Why I wrote a fantasy protagonist who listens instead of fights"
  - "Blockchain proof of authorship: why I put my manuscript on Ethereum"
  - Behind-the-scenes of the world (the seven regions, the crystal systems)
- These serve dual purpose: SEO content + shareable social media material.

**Social Media Setup**
- Claim handles on: TikTok, Instagram, Twitter/X, Threads.
- Don't try to be everywhere. Pick 1–2 platforms and commit.
- **Recommended primary: TikTok (BookTok) + Instagram (Bookstagram).**
- Content approach: reader-focused, not author-advice. Show the world, not the writing process. Short clips of lore, aesthetics, mood boards, character art.

### Month 3: Pre-Launch Heat

**Pre-Order Setup**
- List the ebook for pre-order on Amazon (KDP allows up to 90 days).
- List on other retailers via Draft2Digital.
- Pre-orders count toward Day 1 sales rank — this is critical for Amazon algorithm visibility.

**Newsletter Swaps**
- Find 5–10 authors in epic fantasy / literary fantasy with similar audience sizes.
- Offer to feature their book in your newsletter if they feature yours.
- Platforms for finding swap partners: BookFunnel, StoryOrigin.

**Paid Newsletter Promos (Pre-Order Push)**
- Book a promotion with one or more of these for launch week:
  - **The Fussy Librarian** (~$30–60)
  - **Robin Reads** (~$40–75)
  - **Written Word Media** (~$50–150)
  - Save **BookBub Featured Deal** for post-launch (they prefer books with existing reviews).

**Goodreads Giveaway**
- Run a Goodreads giveaway (print copies) 4–6 weeks before launch. Costs ~$119 + book copies.
- Generates "want to read" adds, which signal demand.

## 5. Launch Month (Month 4)

### Launch Week (Days 1–7)

**Day 1: Go Live**
- Publish ebook and paperback on Amazon KDP.
- Publish paperback and hardcover on IngramSpark.
- Publish ebook wide via Draft2Digital (Apple, Kobo, B&N, Google Play).
- Email your entire list: "The book is live."
- Post on all social media channels.
- ARC team: remind them to post reviews today.

**Day 1–3: Review Push**
- Personally reach out to every ARC reader who hasn't posted yet.
- Goal: **20+ reviews on Amazon within 72 hours.** This is the threshold where the algorithm starts recommending your book. 50+ reviews is the real target within 30 days.

**Day 3–5: Paid Promotion**
- Paid newsletter promos fire (scheduled in Month 3).
- If budget allows, launch Amazon Sponsored Product ads (see Budget section).

**Day 7: Momentum Email**
- Send a second email to your list: "First week results + thank you."
- Include a specific ask: "If you've read it, please leave a review."

### Rest of Launch Month

**Amazon Ads**
- Start with $5–10/day budget on Sponsored Products.
- Target: comparable fantasy authors (Patrick Rothfuss, Robin Hobb, Ursula K. Le Guin, Fonda Lee, N.K. Jemisin — readers of literary-leaning fantasy).
- Target ACoS: 50–70% is acceptable for a debut. You're buying visibility, not immediate profit.
- Monitor daily for first 2 weeks, then weekly.

**Social Media Cadence**
- Post 3–5x/week during launch month.
- Mix: reader reactions, quotes from the book, lore snippets, behind-the-scenes, direct "book is available" calls to action.
- Engage with every comment and share.

## 6. Post-Launch Phase (Months 5–12)

### Month 5–6: Consolidate

**Optimize Amazon Ads**
- By now you have 30 days of ad data. Kill underperforming keywords, increase bids on winners.
- Add Sponsored Brand ads if budget allows.
- Target ACoS below 50% by Month 6.

**Price Promotion**
- Run a $0.99 Kindle Countdown Deal for 48 hours.
- Stack with paid newsletter promos (Fussy Librarian, Robin Reads, Written Word Media).
- Email your list about the deal.
- Goal: spike sales rank, get new readers, drive reviews.

**Apply for BookBub Featured Deal**
- BookBub is the most powerful promotional tool in indie publishing.
- They're selective — you need 20+ reviews and a professional cover to be accepted.
- Apply for a Featured Deal in Fantasy at $0.99. If rejected, apply again next month. Most authors are rejected several times before acceptance.

**Audiobook Production**
- Begin audiobook production.
- Options:
  - **ACX/Audible**: Royalty-share deals (no upfront cost, but you split royalties 50/50 with narrator). Or pay per finished hour ($200–400/PFH for quality narrators).
  - **Findaway Voices**: Higher upfront cost but you keep more royalties and distribute wide.
  - You've already drafted outreach to Jeff Hayes — pursue this. A good narrator transforms fantasy audiobooks.
- Audiobooks are the fastest-growing format. Sanderson calls them "a quiet revolution."

### Month 7–9: Expand

**Library Outreach**
- IngramSpark distribution makes your book available to libraries.
- Directly contact local libraries and request they stock it.
- Use LibraryThing Early Reviewers for additional exposure.

**Bookstore Outreach**
- Visit local independent bookstores in person. Bring copies (ordered from IngramSpark).
- Offer consignment deals or ask if they'll order through Ingram.
- A 55% wholesale discount on IngramSpark makes your book attractive to bookstores.

**Convention / Event Circuit**
- Research fantasy/sci-fi conventions in your region.
- Apply for author tables or panel participation.
- Bring print copies, bookmarks, business cards with QR code to the website.

**Cross-Promotion Scaling**
- By now you should have 1,000+ email subscribers.
- Increase newsletter swap frequency.
- Consider running a multi-author fantasy giveaway via BookFunnel.

### Month 10–12: Evaluate & Plan

**Assess Performance**
- Review total sales, revenue, ad spend, ROI.
- Review email list size and engagement rates.
- Review Amazon rank and review count.

**Sequel Decision**
- If sales support it (1,000+ copies in Year 1), begin writing Book 2.
- The single most effective marketing tool for Book 1 is publishing Book 2.
- Most indie author careers take off after 3–5 books.

**Agent Outreach (Optional)**
- If sales are strong, approach literary agents for:
  - Foreign translation rights
  - Film/TV adaptation rights (you have the screenplay ready)
  - Potential traditional deal for wider distribution
- Come with data: "I've sold X copies independently, here's my platform, here's the screenplay."

## 7. Marketing Channels

### Ranked by Priority

| Priority | Channel | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Email list** | Free–$30/mo | The engine. Everything else feeds this. |
| 2 | **Amazon Ads** | $150–300/mo | Primary paid discovery channel. |
| 3 | **BookTok / TikTok** | Free | Highest organic reach for fantasy. Show the world, not the process. |
| 4 | **Paid newsletter promos** | $30–150/promo | BookBub, Fussy Librarian, Robin Reads. Stack with price drops. |
| 5 | **Instagram / Bookstagram** | Free | Visual platform. Mood boards, cover reveals, quote cards. |
| 6 | **Goodreads** | Free–$119 | Giveaways, reader engagement, reviews. |
| 7 | **Reddit (r/Fantasy)** | Free | Participate genuinely. Self-promo rules are strict — follow them. |
| 8 | **Author website SEO** | Free (already built) | Long-term organic traffic. You're ahead of 95% of indie authors here. |
| 9 | **Local events & bookstores** | $50–200/event | In-person sales, signings, convention tables. |

### What NOT to Do
- Don't spread across 8 social platforms doing mediocre work on all of them. Pick 2.
- Don't buy followers or fake reviews. Ever.
- Don't spend money on ads before you have 20+ organic reviews.
- Don't blog about writing advice. Blog about the world and the story. Readers are your audience, not other authors.

## Unique Differentiators

What makes MATC stand out from the 4,000 fantasy novels published per month:

1. **Blockchain proof of authorship** — No other indie fantasy novel has this. It's a story in itself.
2. **The anti-Chosen-One protagonist** — Aelo listens. He doesn't fight. This subverts the genre in a marketable way.
3. **Scientific metaphor system** — Crystal symmetry, geological vocabulary, resonance physics as magic. This appeals to readers who want fantasy that respects intelligence.
4. **Complete media package** — Website ecosystem, companion story, screenplay trilogy, world bible. You're not pitching a book — you're pitching a franchise.
5. **AI transparency** — Your documented, honest approach to AI as a tool (not a ghost author) is rare and positions you ahead of an industry-wide conversation that's only getting louder.
