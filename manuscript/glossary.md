# GLOSSARY

_A guide to the world, its peoples, and its magic._

---

## People & Titles

**Aelo** (ah-EH-lo) — A boy from a nameless village at the edge of the Canopy. The name means "breath of remembering" in the old tongue of the Vael Guard.

**Baara** — The birth name of The Knife. A sound without meaning in any known language.

**Cloud Singers** — Women of the Cloud People who carry the Song in their blood, passed from mother to daughter through an unbroken maternal line stretching back to King Fletcher.

**Essa** — A name from the oldest stories. A Guide of the Sea People.

**Fletcher, King** — The first mortal given the ability to hear the world's song. He taught the seven disciplines to humanity and, in the age of wars, Sang the Great Elder Stone into existence — pouring every memory he possessed into the stone and dissolving into the world's memory.

**Halla** — The village schoolteacher who delivers the state-approved history of the realm.

**Jalo Soturi** (YAH-lo so-TOO-ree) — Aelo's caretaker. A limping, scarred man known in the village as a passable healer and a steady drinker. His name means "noble warrior."

**King Aldric** — A just king who ruled the Cloud Kingdom before its fall. He possessed the Know and believed magic was a shared resource.

**King Varas** — The ruler of the realm. No one alive has seen him in over a decade. He rules through surrogates, his soldiers, and his son.

**Knower, The** — A practitioner of the Know, often compelled into service during the Knower Ceremonies.

**Laine** (Finnish: "wave") — A young woman of the Sea People and a rare Guide who can sense the trajectory of the future.

**Maera, Queen** — Wife of King Aldric. A Cloud Singer of the old kingdom.

**Ming Men** — Scholars, philosophers, and psychics of the Ming region. Their advanced practice of the Know allows them to enter another person's memories and walk through them.

**Mira** — A warrior-monk of the Pure Ones. Seventy years old. She has not spoken in twenty years and teaches through gesture and demonstration.

**Prince Vero** — A prince of the Cloud Kingdom. Kind, open, and said to carry a faint echo of the Song in his blood.

**Pure Ones** — The warrior-monks of the Canopy, who follow a strict code of honor, silence, and service.

**Seers** — Elders of the Cloud People, so named not for prophecy but for the patterns they see from the heights that others cannot.

**Sera** — A former captain of the Vael Guard, silver-haired, who administered the oath to new members.

**Sereth** — A figure from the oldest stories. An information broker who appears in every region under a different name and face.

**The Knife** — The King's enforcer. Tall, lean, fast. He earned the name at twelve. He carries a single smooth stone as his weapon.

**The Warden** — The man who runs the prison where magic is drained from captives. Not cruel — bureaucratic.

**Torvus** — A Molder who lives alone in a cavern he carved himself over forty years. Enormous, gregarious, and completely numb below the ears.

**Vael Guard** — An ancient order of warrior-healers sworn to protect King Fletcher's bloodline across generations. They were both fighters and Healers, absorbing the phantom pain of every wound they mended.

## The Seven Disciplines

_Each discipline is a way of listening to a different kind of memory._

**The Know** — Listening to the memory of living things — their truth, intent, emotion, and history. A Knower can feel whether someone is lying. **Cost:** Knowers absorb what they read, risking emotional contamination.

**The Mold** — Speaking to stone, earth, metal, and wood — asking it to remember a different shape. **Cost:** Molders slowly lose physical sensation, beginning with the fingers.

**The Heal** — Reminding flesh and bone of wholeness. A Healer coaxes the body to remember what it was before the wound. **Cost:** Healers take the memory of the wound's pain into themselves. A Healer cannot heal himself.

**The Move** — Asking space and air to remember a different arrangement of things. **Cost:** Movers lose spatial memory — their sense of where they are in the world.

**The Guide** — Listening to the memory of paths — where things have been and where they are going. **Cost:** Guides lose their memory of home. They can find anything except where they came from.

**The Burn** — Speaking to fire and heat — awakening the world's memory of the first flame. **Cost:** Burners lose emotional warmth. They become cold inside — detached, unable to feel joy or love with the same intensity.

**The Sing** — Speaking to all memory at once. Harmony. The music the world makes when every memory resonates together. The rarest discipline. **Cost:** Everything. To Sing is to pour your entire self into the world's memory.

## Magic & Phenomena

**Blood Moon** — The astronomical event that precedes the Blue Sun each year.

**Blood Vine** — A parasitic plant that grows only in darkness and feeds on magical residue. In nature, it attaches to Elder Stones and slowly siphons ambient memory. Under King Varas, it has been weaponized as a conduit between living people and Elder Stones for forced extraction.

**Blood-mark** — An amber mark that sometimes appears on the inner wrist, visible only in certain light. It pulses with a rhythm like a heartbeat. Its origin and meaning are tied to the oldest orders of the realm.

**Blue Sun** — A celestial event that occurs once each year, during which the Knower Ceremonies are traditionally held.

**Calibration Stone** — A small, inert Elder Stone stripped of capacity, used by surveyors to measure ambient magical density in a region.

**Cloudmilk** — A substance distilled from condensation gathered at extreme altitude. It has mild restorative properties and is a controlled substance under the current regime.

**Elder Stones** — Ancient stones that exist at the intersection of all seven disciplines. They are the world's oldest memories crystallized into physical form. They can store memory willingly given, and under Varas, extract memory by force.

**Great Elder Stone, The** — The most powerful object in the world. Created by King Fletcher through the Sing. Hidden among the clouds, it sings a single, perfect note that has not faded in three hundred years.

**Greensong, The** — A form of trance-running practiced by warriors of the Canopy, where they synchronize their heartbeat with the forest's rhythm. A Greensinger can travel at extraordinary speed for days without rest. When they stop, the debt hits all at once.

**Knower Ceremony (The Knowing)** — Once a year, under the Blue Sun, children are tested for magical ability. In the old days, it was a celebration. Under Varas, it has become an engine of extraction.

**The Drain** — The process of forcibly ripping memory from a living person through Blood Vine and Elder Stones, then consuming it as magical fuel. Stolen memory is corrupted and degrades with each transfer.

**The Prophecy** — Carved into the base of the Great Elder Stone in the language of memory: _One will come who carries the Song in his blood. He will hear the stone. He will find the note. And in Singing, he will remember what the world forgot._

**The Weave** — A discipline unique to the Desert People: the art of braiding weak fragments of multiple disciplines together into something functional. The most technically demanding form of magic in the world.

## Regions

_The world is divided into seven regions, each defined by its primary elemental memory, separated by massive walls of crystallized magic called **The Rememberings**._

**The Canopy (Lehti)** — An ancient, vast forest where life is vertical. The people live in the mid-canopy, connected by rope bridges and carved stairways. The forest floor is sacred, reserved for the dead. **Element:** Wood / Life. **Magic:** The Know, The Greensong.

**The Clouds (Pilvi)** — The Cloud People live on floating formations of stone held aloft by ancient Move magic so old the air itself remembers them being there. The view is endless. The air is thin. **Element:** Air / Sky. **Magic:** The Sing (ancestral), The Move, The Guide.

**The Core (Syvä)** — A rocky continent of canyons and mesas on the surface, but the true Core is underground — cities carved deep beneath the crust, lit by bioluminescent fungi and crystal veins. **Element:** Earth / Stone. **Magic:** The Mold.

**The Desert (Kuiva)** — An endless expanse of sand where the world's memory has been damaged. Magic behaves unpredictably here. The people are nomadic, following underground water sources. **Element:** Sand / Absence. **Magic:** The Weave.

**The Ming (Mieli)** — Rolling hills and still lakes, perpetually shrouded in mist. The land itself is semi-conscious. The mist is the region thinking. **Element:** Mind / Thought. **Magic:** The Know (advanced), psychic disciplines.

**The Murkr** (Old Norse: "darkness") — The convergence point at the center of the world where all seven regions meet. Once the brightest place on earth, it has been drained into perpetual twilight. The castle sits at its heart.

**The Sea (Bahar)** — A vast coastline around a dark, cold ocean. The Sea People live on shore and on floating platforms anchored to the seabed. They are the world's best Healers. **Element:** Water / Tide. **Magic:** The Guide, The Heal.

**The Volcano (Kuuma)** — A chain of active and dormant volcanoes. The Volcano People are the world's smiths, with thick, heat-adapted skin. **Element:** Fire / Heat. **Magic:** The Burn.

**The Rememberings** — Natural walls of crystallized elemental magic that separate the seven regions. Each wall is a monument to its element.

## Creatures

**Armored Ants** — Ancient insects that march in slow columns through the loam. Patient, enduring, older than human memory.

**Flash Rat** — A hairless, fox-sized creature from the Core's deep caverns. It navigates by scent and vibration through extraordinary wire-like whiskers. When it detects a magical signature, its body produces a bright pulse of blue-white bioluminescence. Bred by the Mrak Patrol as trackers — relentless, single-minded, incapable of being redirected once it has acquired a scent.

**Hollow Whale** — The largest living things in the world. They surface occasionally in the deep ocean off Bahar. Their memory is so old it resists all communion — the Know cannot read them. The Sea People consider them sacred and unknowable.

**Hopper** — A small, common brown bird of the Canopy.

**Saddle Bird** — A large avian beast used for medium-distance travel, primarily by the Cloud People. Temperamental. Bonds with a single rider.

## Organizations & Forces

**Mrak Patrol** — King Varas's military force. They guard the sealed gates between regions and enforce the King's authority. Their armor is dark, stamped with the double-stone sigil. The shock troops are overwhelmingly Volcano People.

**Vael Guard** — See entry under People & Titles.

## Herbs & Remedies

**Blackthorn** — For nerve pain. Good for shaking and withdrawal.

**Breedlebuck** — For inflammation. Reminds the body's tissue that swelling is not its natural state.

**Fiddleroot** — For fever and trembling. Interrupts the body's rehearsal of fighting by offering a newer memory of being cool.

**Ironbark** — For bone pain. Honors the bone's record of carrying weight, so the bone stops rehearsing the pain because the pain has been heard.

**Milkwillow** — A healing herb kept among Jalo's stores.

**Root Wine** — A fermented alcoholic drink. Jalo's anesthetic of choice.

**Vigor Leaf** — For mental fog. Used in Jalo's morning tincture after difficult nights.

---

## Transport & Technology

**Enchanted Communication Devices** — Paired Elder Stone fragments that transmit short bursts of emotional memory over distance. Not words — feelings.

**Flying Ships** — Vessels held aloft by Elder Stones in the hull and Move magic through the mast. Rare and expensive.

**Weave-Roads** — Desert trade routes where Weavers have braided small magics into the sand, creating paths that are always cool, always flat, and always point toward water. Invisible to anyone who doesn't know the Weave.

## Key Phrases

**"I am the wall between the fire and the song. I will burn before the song goes silent."** — The oath of the Vael Guard.

**"Breath of remembering"** — The meaning of the name Aelo.

**The Listening** — The Canopy ceremony in which twelve-year-old children descend to the sacred forest floor for the first time and sit in the dark among the roots and the dead, trying to hear the Know.
