Make your tinctures by simply filling a jar 1/3 to 1/2 full of your herbs (1/2 full makes for a stronger brew; do not pack in the herbs), and pour in just enough hot water to get them wet. Then, fill the jar to the top with alcohol, seal, and store for 3 weeks in a dry, cool place (shake daily).

What is a herbal preparation?

Herbal teas, or tisanes, are the resultant liquid of extracting herbs into water, though they are made in a few different ways. Infusions are hot water extracts of herbs, such as chamomile or mint, through steeping. Decoctions are the long-term boiled extracts, usually of harder substances like roots or bark.
