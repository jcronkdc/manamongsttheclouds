# AI Transparency Statement & Proof of Ownership

## AI Transparency Statement

*Man Amongst the Clouds* was written by Justin Cronk. The story, characters, world, magic system, prose, and every creative decision in this novel are entirely his own work — developed over nine years, beginning in March 2017.

### What AI Was NOT Used For
- Writing prose, dialogue, or narrative content
- Creating characters, plot, or world-building concepts
- Generating the magic system or its rules
- Producing any creative content that appears in the final manuscript

### What AI Was Used For
AI tools were used in a **support capacity** — the same role a copywriter, developmental editor, or continuity reader would fill:

- **Continuity auditing:** Checking for inconsistencies across 48 chapters and 153,000 words (character ages, timeline, magic system rules, object tracking)
- **Copywriting:** Drafting marketing materials (book descriptions, author bios, query letters, keyword optimization)
- **Structural feedback:** Identifying plot holes, pacing issues, and thread payoffs
- **Formatting:** Building compilation scripts to assemble chapter files into manuscript format
- **Publishing logistics:** Research on self-publishing best practices, pricing, distribution

In every case, AI served as a tool — not a co-author. The same way a carpenter uses a power drill but builds the house.

### Verification
This manuscript has been submitted for AI content detection analysis. You can verify the human authorship using any of these services:

- **GPTZero** — [gptzero.me](https://gptzero.me) — The most widely respected AI detection tool. Upload any chapter or the full manuscript.
- **Originality.ai** — [originality.ai](https://originality.ai) — Comprehensive AI detection with detailed scoring.
- **Winston AI** — [gowinston.ai](https://gowinston.ai) — Optimized for long-form content.

We encourage skeptical readers to test any passage. The prose style of this novel — dense, metaphor-rich, recursive, deeply personal — is not the kind of writing AI produces. It's the kind of writing that takes nine years.
