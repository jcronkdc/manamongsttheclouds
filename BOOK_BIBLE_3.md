## VIII. EXISTING WRITTEN SCENES & PASSAGES

These are the narrative passages you've already drafted. They are your strongest written material and represent the voice and tone of the book.

### Scene 1: The Knower Ceremony (Narrated History)

> "Today was the day of the blue sun. As always it was proceeded by the blood moon. Where once there was celebration and salutations, celebrating the powers of the children, and what they may become. Had now become a somber affair. Families sitting quietly at dinner trying not to think about the day to come. During the peak of the blood moon, a century sent by the king would stand guard. Knocking on the door at dawn before the rise of the blue sun and escorting the eligible children to the ceremony square. Any resistance was met with certain death. It had been a long time since anyone resisted. The cost was never worth it."

### Scene 2: The Dog Scene — Assassination Attempt at Court (Longest Written Passage)

A fully drafted scene in which a man attempts to assassinate King Varas at a feast. The King severs his guard dog's head, then the assassin's, and uses Heal & Mold magic to graft the man's head onto the dog's body. General Baara ("The Knife") cuts out the man's tongue. The King forces the man-dog to identify conspirators by going into the crowd. Lord Kirik is implicated, has his own tongue cut out, and is forced to place the assassin's severed tongue back into the dog-man's mouth. Five conspirators (including Lord Kirik) are all turned into dogs and sent back to their families as living reminders. The King delivers a speech on what it means to be a dog.

_(This scene showcases Varas's sadistic intelligence, dark humor, and absolute power. It's the most complete piece of prose in the notes.)_

### Scene 3: The Detangler's Monologue (Voice/Dialogue)

> "What does it mean to detangle magic? Oh boy, that's not a thing a man can figure out with only a few words. Detangling is not necessarily a thing you do, it's more like a thing you feel. You have to feel the magic inside them as a whole first. After a while you begin to discern certain shapes and strengths and weaknesses and natural borders. I call this 'partitioned' magic..."

### Scene 4: Oracle Exchange with Nim

> Nim asks, "Am I the one in the prophecy? I'm not him, am I?"
> The Oracle answered, "My son, you are exactly who you think you are. It is as you said — right now, you are not him."

## IX. LINEAR PLOT OUTLINE (Proposed Structure)

Based on all your notes, here is a reconstructed linear narrative arc. Items marked _(gap)_ are areas that need to be written or developed.

---

### PART ONE: THE WORLD BEFORE

**Chapter 1 — The Age of Balance**

- The ancient history: magic as a gift from the gods, King Fletcher, the scrolls, the Elder Stone, the prophecy. _(Narrated prologue or woven into early chapters.)_
- Varas's father rules justly. Magic is shared. Excess is rationed.

**Chapter 2 — The Usurper**

- Varas's backstory: jealousy of his brother Vero, his father's suspicion of darkness in him.
- Varas kills (apparently) his father and brother. Seizes the throne.
- _Plot twist seed planted: they are alive in his private chambers, being drained._

**Chapter 3 — The New Order**

- Varas's first council meeting: summons all regional leaders and their best magicians.
- Executes the magicians, adds them to his chamber. Executes the Lords, installs his own men.
- The Knower Ceremony is perverted — becomes a tool of extraction and terror.
- The kingdom descends into fear. Mrak Patrols enforce the "King's Justice."

**Chapter 4 — Nim and Jalo**

- Introduce Nim as a gentle, sensitive child living in a remote area with his caretaker Jalo Soturi.
- Jalo is the town drunk with a limp, a cane (staff), and facial scars. Overprotective.
- Nim is small for his age (stunted by herbs). Doesn't know why.
- Jalo's cabinet: handmade, stone hearth, trapping. Simple, hidden life.
- _(Gap: daily life, their relationship, the tension of Jalo's secrecy.)_

**Chapter 5 — Signs of Power**

- Nim begins showing signs of unusual magic despite the suppression herbs.
- _(Gap: what triggers this? A Knower Ceremony approaches? An encounter?)_
- Nim rebels against Jalo's overprotectiveness. Resentment builds.

**Chapter 6 — The Discovery**

- Nim discovers Jalo has been feeding him herbs to suppress his magic.
- Confrontation between Nim and Jalo.
- _(Gap: does Nim stop taking the herbs? Does his growth begin to catch up painfully?)_

## X. OPEN QUESTIONS & GAPS TO RESOLVE

1. **What is Jalo's true identity?** He shares a scar with Nim. He's a former warrior. The Knife recognizes him. Is he royalty? A guardian of the old order?
2. **What is "The Snake's" role?** Named but undeveloped.
3. **General Baara's arc:** He's the King's son AND enforcer. How does he feel about his father? Is there any conflict before he's drained?
4. **Nim's parentage:** Is he directly descended from King Fletcher? From Varas's brother Vero? How was he hidden?
5. **The Murkr:** What is it? Where is it? What happens there?
6. **Nim's training journey:** Each region needs a mentor, a trial, and a lesson.
7. **The prisoner who escapes:** Is this a subplot? A separate character who joins Nim?
8. **Transport:** Flying ships, wormholes — how does travel work in this world?
9. **Currency:** Noted but undefined.
10. **The suicide attempt timing:** If Jalo is dead, who saves Nim on the cloud?
11. **Schools and education system:** Subjects defined but no scenes written.
12. **Magic-free zones:** People who see magic as evil — potential allies or enemies?
13. **The enchanted cellphone device:** How does communication work?
14. **Nim's empathic ability:** He can feel Jalo's pain. Is this part of his unique power set?
