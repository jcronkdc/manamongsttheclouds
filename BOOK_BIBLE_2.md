## V. CHARACTERS

### NIM (Protagonist)
- **Nature:** Gentle, sensitive child. Cries easily when hurt.
- **Background:** Raised by Jalo Soturi in hiding. Fed herbs since childhood to suppress his magic (which also stunted his growth). Growth will catch up painfully once he stops.
- **Arc:** Doesn't believe he is the prophecy's chosen one. Attempts suicide by leaping from the cloud so no more people will die for him — saved by Jalo.
- **Prophecy connection:** Likely of royal blood (King Fletcher's line). The "man amongst the clouds."
- **Key Oracle moment:** Nim asks, *"Am I the one in the prophecy? I'm not him, am I?"* The Oracle answers: *"My son, you are exactly who you think you are. It is as you said — right now, you are not him."*
- **Climax:** Nim sacrifices his entire essence to bring those in the King's chamber back to consciousness so they can collectively defeat the King. This happens after the King has drained his own son, Baara.
- **Possible status:** A villein — not free, owned by a lord.

### JALO SOTURI (Nim's Caretaker / Guardian)
- **Name meaning:** "Noble Warrior" (Finnish)
- **Role:** Herbal healer trained in battle magic. Former warrior. Nim's guardian in hiding.
- **Abilities:** Move & Mold magic. Uses herbs to conceal & suppress magic.
- **Physical:** Walks with a cane (actually a staff). Old knee injury he refuses to heal — tied to a painful memory he won't forget. Bandaged so Nim never sees. Significant facial scarring from fire (possibly self-inflicted to hide identity). Has the same scar as Nim.
- **Personality:** Drinks to prevent Nim from feeling his pain (Nim is empathic). Town drunk. Hands tremble. Nightmares. Withholding a secret. Lost entire family → overprotective → Nim rebels and resents him.
- **Home:** Handmade furniture, stone hearth, trapping equipment.
- **Key plot beats:**
  - When Nim is kidnapped, Jalo sobers up → Nim feels all his pain.
  - Nim discovers Jalo has been suppressing his magic with herbs.
  - Jalo always heals Nim but never himself.
  - Jalo fights The Knife after Nim is captured. The Knife discovers Jalo's true identity. Jalo is killed by The Knife shortly after.

### KING VARAS (Antagonist)
- **Personality:** Extreme introvert (INTJ archetype). Uses a puppet/surrogate to attend meetings. Fear of death. Avoids expending energy. Sees only a few advisors.
- **Backstory:** Was set to inherit throne. Father sensed darkness in him. Father and brother Vero both had magic. Varas killed them both — **plot twist: they're still alive, constantly drained, entombed in Varas's private chambers.**
- **Father's philosophy:** Magic for everyone. Too much power to one person = corruption.
- **Varas's rule:** Summoned all regional leaders and their best magicians. Executed the magicians and added them to his chamber to drain. Executed the Lords, replaced with his own men. Treats his chamber like a "wonder closet" collection. Careless with power — some die when he drains too much. Vampire for magic.
- **Decline:** Complacent over time. Rarely left castle. Weakening. Earliest drains dying of old age. Desperation made him crueler. Threw parties where guests drank lifeblood of slaves. Began training 7th sons in secret.
- **Appearance:** Changed drastically over decades — in size, form, and color. Hadn't been seen for over a decade by people outside the castle.

### GENERAL BAARA / "THE KNIFE" (King's Enforcer)
- Son of King Varas
- Primary enforcer and executioner
- Flings a stone as weapon
- Eventually drained by his own father
- Kills Jalo after discovering his true identity

## VI. SETTING DETAILS

### The Castle
- **After Varas:** Set deep beyond the iron gate. Each tower guarded by a great bird. All who enter must make a blood donation.

### The Mrak Patrol
- Captain had tip of a narwhal horn in his helmet. Narwhal harvested off the coast of **Bahar**, where the bloody seas met the cold depths. Grief stone beast plates for the boxer.
- Every region has a roving patrol authorized to enact the **"King's Justice."**

### Schools
- Train children from babies to adulthood
- Subjects: History of the Realm (King Varas version + hidden version), Philosophy of Magic
- Various university styles
- Special forces selection and training

### Prisons
- Rows of people standing, magic slowly drained into massive Elder Stones
- Burlap bags over heads
- Weak healing magic keeps them barely alive (stones on heart and head)
- *(A prisoner escapes and steals stones from other inmates, combining them for greater strength)*
- Draining, wiping, reorganization

### The Black Market
- Potions, Spells, Enchantments, Magic suppression, Magic expulsion, Triddles

### Transport
- Flying ships *(noted, to be developed)*
- Wormholes *(noted)*
